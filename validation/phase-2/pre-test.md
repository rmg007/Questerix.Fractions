## Items for All Students (K, Grade 1, Grade 2)

---

**Item 1** _(tests SK-02, linked to H-01)_

> "Circle the shape that shows ONE HALF."

```
[ Shape A: circle divided into 2 equal parts, 1 shaded ]
[ Shape B: circle divided into 3 equal parts, 1 shaded ]
[ Shape C: uncut circle, fully unshaded ]
```

Response: A / B / C / NR  
Correct: **A**

---

**Item 2** _(tests SK-08 / identification of one-fourth, linked to H-01)_

> "Circle the shape that shows ONE FOURTH."

```
[ Shape A: square divided into 2 equal parts, 1 shaded ]
[ Shape B: square divided into 4 equal parts, 1 shaded ]
[ Shape C: square divided into 3 unequal parts, 1 region shaded ]
```

Response: A / B / C / NR  
Correct: **B**

---

**Item 3** _(tests SK-11, linked to H-02)_

**Item 4** _(tests SK-16, linked to H-02)_

> "Look at this circle. Draw lines that split it into FOUR equal parts."

**Item 5** _(tests SK-23 / MC-WHB-02, linked to H-03)_

> "Which is bigger — one half or one fourth? Circle the bigger one."

```
[ Left: rectangle with 1/2 shaded, labeled "1/2" ]
[ Right: same-size rectangle with 1/4 shaded, labeled "1/4" ]
```

Response: 1/2 / 1/4 / NR  
Correct: **1/2**

---

**Item 6** _(tests SK-22, same-denominator comparison, linked to H-03)_

> "Which is bigger — two fourths or three fourths? Circle the bigger one."

```
[ Left: rectangle divided into 4 parts, 2 shaded, labeled "2/4" ]
[ Right: same-size rectangle divided into 4 parts, 3 shaded, labeled "3/4" ]
```

Response: 2/4 / 3/4 / NR  
Correct: **3/4**

## Item 7 — Grade 1 and Grade 2 Only (K students skip)

> **K students:** Give the K Substitute below instead.

**Item 7 — Grade 1+** _(tests SK-27, number-line placement, linked to H-03; restricted to Grade 1+ per playtest-protocol.md §3.1 audit §2.4)_

> "Look at this fraction strip. Put a mark where ONE HALF belongs on the line from 0 to 1."

```
[ Horizontal number line, 10 cm wide ]
[ Left endpoint labeled "0", right endpoint labeled "1" ]
[ Blank space above the line for the child to mark ]
```

Scored correct if mark is within ±10% of strip length from the 0.5 midpoint.  
Score: Correct / Incorrect / NR
