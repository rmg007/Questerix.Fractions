**What I'm asking:**

- Three short play sessions over about two weeks (about 15 minutes each).

**What it involves:**

- No accounts, no logins. The game runs in a browser (iPad or Chromebook works well).
- No data leaves the device — I just take handwritten notes about what kids find tricky.
- I'll provide a simple one-page consent form before we start.
- There's a small thank-you gift at the end (sticker pack or $5 bookstore card — not tied to how long they play).

**What I'm looking for:**

I'd like a mix of kids who already know "one half" and kids who don't. Any grade from K to 2 is welcome. About 8–10 kids total across both groups.

This is an informal pilot — not a formal study. The goal is simply to see if the game teaches what it's supposed to teach, so I can decide whether to keep building it.
