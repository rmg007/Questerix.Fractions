# Teacher Briefing Script -- Phase 3 (30-Minute Brief)

Use this script when briefing the classroom teacher before Phase 3 begins. Adapt the wording as needed. Budget 30 minutes; bold items are required, the rest can be shortened if the teacher is pressed for time.

---

## Part 1: What the app is (5 min)

"Questerix Fractions is a browser math game for K-2 students. It teaches fractions -- halves, thirds, fourths -- using a drag-and-tap mechanic. Students split shapes, identify fractions, and compare them. The game gives immediate feedback. There are no scores shown to students -- just 'correct' or 'try again.'"

Show the teacher the Menu scene and click through one Level 1 question so they can see what students will experience.

"Sessions are 15 minutes. The app tracks which questions a student answered correctly and stores that on the device -- nothing goes to a server."

---

## Part 2: What students will be asked to do (5 min)

"Each student will play three sessions over [scheduled dates]. Before the first session, each student does a short paper activity -- about 10 minutes, pencil and paper. After the third session, they do a parallel paper activity at least one day later. That's it."

"The paper activities look like a short worksheet. You don't need to grade them -- the researcher scores them. Your job is to hand them out, read the prompts aloud if needed, and collect them."

Hand the teacher the printed pre-test and post-test so they can see what they look like.

---

## Part 3: What to write down (10 min)

**This is the most important part of the briefing.**

"Each observer has a printed observation template for each student. During the session, each observer watches 5-6 students. They are not teaching -- they are watching and taking notes."

Walk through the observation template together. Point out:

- The timing codes: P = long pause, V = verbal statement, F = frustration, H = help-seeking.
- The intervention log: "Every time you break silence, write it down. The default is to stay quiet."
- The debrief questions at the end: "What was your favorite part? What was tricky?"

"If a student wants to stop, let them stop. Write 'withdrew at session N' and move on."

---

## Part 4: Data export (5 min)

"At the end of each session, click the Backup My Progress button in the app for each device. Save the file with the student's pseudonym in the name. Transfer to [researcher's machine / USB drive] before students leave."

Hand the teacher the data-export-checklist.md printout. Walk through it once.

---

## Part 5: Questions (5 min)

Open floor. Common questions:

Q: What if a student finishes the 15 minutes and wants to keep playing?
A: "It's fine if they keep playing. Just note the time when the 15 minutes ended."

Q: What if the app crashes?
A: "Hard-reload the page. The progress is stored on the device, so it should resume. Note the time lost."

Q: Can parents watch?
A: "That's your call as the teacher. If parents observe, ask them not to coach their child."

Q: Will I see the results?
A: "Yes, I'll share an anonymized summary after analysis."

---

Thank the teacher and confirm the schedule for Sessions 1, 2, and 3.
