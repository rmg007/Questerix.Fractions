# DIFFERENTIATION GUIDE — GRADE 1 FRACTIONS

> _"Equity is not giving every child the same thing. Equity is giving every child what they need. A first-grade classroom is 20+ different minds; a great curriculum anticipates this."_

---

## FOUNDATIONAL COMMITMENT

This curriculum is built for ALL Grade 1 learners. Not for the "average" or the "typical," but for the REAL ones in YOUR classroom: the English Language Learners, the students with disabilities, the gifted, the struggling, the anxious, the confident, the quiet, the loud, the outgoing, the inward.

Differentiation is not a set of accommodations added on top. It is **woven into the design**.

---

## THE FIVE DIMENSIONS OF DIFFERENTIATION

We differentiate along five dimensions:

1. **Linguistic** — for English Language Learners and language-delayed students
2. **Cognitive** — for students above or below grade level expectations
3. **Physical** — for students with motor, visual, or sensory needs
4. **Cultural** — for students from varied cultural backgrounds
5. **Emotional** — for students with anxiety, trauma, or attention differences

---

## LINGUISTIC DIFFERENTIATION

### For English Language Learners (ELLs):

**At all levels of proficiency:**

- Pair with a bilingual buddy when possible
- Use gesture consistently for key concepts (half = hand chop; whole = hand circle)
- Provide visual supports alongside all text
- Allow non-verbal demonstration as valid responses

**For beginning ELLs:**

- Focus on picture-based tasks
- Provide vocabulary cards in home language
- Accept one-word answers or pointing
- Don't require complete sentences

**For intermediate ELLs:**

- Provide sentence frames: "I halved the **\_**." "Two halves make a **\_**."
- Pair oral and written vocabulary practice
- Encourage labeling with words and pictures

**For advanced ELLs:**

- Support academic vocabulary (equal, partition, compose)
- Encourage extended explanations
- May participate in all tasks with minimal accommodation

### Translations Provided:

Key vocabulary translated in `materials/BILINGUAL_VOCABULARY.md` into:

- Spanish
- Mandarin
- Arabic
- French
- Vietnamese
- Tagalog
- Haitian Creole
- (Additional languages available on request)

## COGNITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

### For Students Working Above Grade Level:

**Do not:**

- Give them Grade 2 worksheets
- Isolate them from peers
- Push symbolic notation early

**Do:**

- Provide **extension tasks** (see `extensions/`) that deepen rather than accelerate
- Offer leadership roles (peer teaching, assistant to Queen Equallia, Problem Designer)
- Encourage creativity: "Invent a new fraction story" "Design a new Crumb mistake"
- Introduce concepts informally without requiring them: "What do you think a THIRD is?"

**Sample extension tasks:**

- "How many ways can you halve this square?"
- "Create a word problem for your classmates"
- "Teach Crumb how to avoid a specific mistake"
- "If we halved a fourth, what would we get?"

### For Students Working Below Grade Level:

**Do not:**

- Give up or lower expectations permanently
- Segregate into "slow group"
- Skip core content
- Retain based on fraction progress alone

**Do:**

- Provide MORE time at foundational levels
- Use LARGER, more concrete materials
- Pair with supportive peer
- Break activities into smaller chunks
- Confer 1-on-1 more frequently
- Involve family as partners
- Celebrate incremental growth

**Sample supports:**

- Pre-folded paper (child doesn't have to fold)
- Fewer items per task (3 shapes instead of 5)
- Extra check-ins
- Visual step-by-step cards
- Sentence frames for explanation

### For Students with IEPs:

Specific accommodations depend on the IEP. General principles:

- Align with IEP goals (they may differ from general curriculum)
- Provide accommodations consistently
- Coordinate with the special education team
- Document what works

**Common IEP accommodations useful here:**

- Extended time
- Reduced item count
- Alternative response modes (pointing, yes/no questions)
- Peer support
- Sensory breaks
- Manipulatives required (not optional)

### For 504 Plans:

Common accommodations:

- Preferential seating (near instruction)
- Check-ins and refocusing
- Modified materials
- Non-verbal cues

## PHYSICAL DIFFERENTIATION

### For Motor Challenges:

- **Pre-cut / pre-scored** paper materials
- **Larger manipulatives** (larger circles, larger counters)
- **Adaptive scissors** or adult-cut options
- **Velcro-backed** fraction pieces for easier manipulation
- **Magnetic** fraction pieces for vertical work
- **Stabilized** materials (tape to prevent sliding)

### For Visual Differences:

- **Large-print** materials (22+ point font)
- **High-contrast** visuals (black on white, yellow on black)
- **Tactile alternatives** (textured halves vs. fourths)
- **Audio descriptions** of visual tasks
- **Adapted activities** that don't rely on visual discrimination

### For Hearing Differences:

- **Visual schedules** posted at child's station
- **Written instructions** for every oral instruction
- **Sign language interpreters** when available (coordinate with district)
- **Captioned videos** if using digital content
- **Visual attention signals** (flickering light instead of bell)

### For Sensory Sensitivities:

- **Quiet workspace** option (corner, headphones)
- **Sensory break** routines built into day
- **Material alternatives** (avoid specific textures, sounds)
- **Predictable transitions** (warning before change)
- **Reduced overstimulation** in visual environment

## CULTURAL DIFFERENTIATION

### Guiding Principle:

The Halving Kingdom is a **vessel**, not a doctrine. Adapt to your students' cultural contexts.

### Food Adaptations:

Default: pizza. Alternatives:

- Tortillas (Mexican, Central/South American)
- Naan (Indian, South Asian)
- Injera (Ethiopian)
- Pita (Middle Eastern)
- Baguette (French)
- Mochi (Japanese)
- Bao (Chinese)

Use the foods that reflect your classroom.

### Dietary Inclusion:

- Pre-plan for allergies (nut-free, gluten-free, dairy-free)
- Honor religious dietary laws (halal, kosher, vegetarian)
- Communicate with families before any real-food activity
- Always have paper alternatives

### Cultural Narrative Alternatives:

For communities uncomfortable with monarchy themes:

- **The Sharing Village** (cooperative community, not kingdom)
- **The Fair Federation** (democratic, voted leaders)
- **The Equal Nation** (general civic setting)

The mathematical structure is invariant. The setting adapts.

### Name Adaptations:

Character names are translatable. See `narrative/CHARACTER_GUIDE.md` for translations. Teachers may substitute names that reflect their students' heritage.

### Holiday and Celebration Integration:

- Incorporate fraction activities during cultural celebrations (Lunar New Year, Diwali, Eid, Día de los Muertos, Kwanzaa, Christmas, Hanukkah)
- Let children share how their families divide and share food at celebrations
- "Halving" looks different across cultures — honor that

## EMOTIONAL DIFFERENTIATION

### For Students with Anxiety:

- **Low-stakes framing** — activities as "quests" not "tests"
- **Predictable routines** — same structure daily
- **Preview upcoming events** — "On Friday we'll have the Halving Ceremony. Here's what will happen."
- **Private practice** option before public performance
- **Self-regulation tools** — breathing breaks, movement breaks
- **Family communication** about triggers

### For Students with Trauma Histories:

- **Safety first** — establish emotional safety before academic challenge
- **Avoid surprise** — no abrupt story shifts
- **Avoid high-stakes judgment** — Queen Equallia is kind, not scary
- **Monitor narrative** — Lord Notenough can be reframed or minimized for specific children
- **Involve school counselor** for any significant concerns
- **Trauma-informed principles** — safety, trust, choice, collaboration, empowerment

### For Students with Attention Differences (ADHD):

- **Shorter activity segments** (10-15 min, not 30+)
- **Movement breaks** every 15-20 min
- **Active learning** (not passive listening)
- **Novelty** to sustain engagement
- **Clear, concrete tasks**
- **Visual step-by-step guides**
- **Fidget tools** permitted
- **Flexible seating** options

### For Students Who Are Highly Sensitive:

- **Quieter voice** during emotional narrative moments
- **Pre-exposure** to big ceremonial events
- **Opt-out option** for big performances (they can participate differently)
- **Reassurance** that their quieter presence is valued

## UDL (UNIVERSAL DESIGN FOR LEARNING) INTEGRATION

This curriculum follows UDL principles:

### Multiple Means of Engagement:

- Narrative engages emotional investment
- Characters provide identification options
- Choice of tasks where appropriate
- Celebration built-in

### Multiple Means of Representation:

- Oral read-alouds AND written stories AND pictures
- Concrete materials AND drawings AND words
- Videos (when useful) AND live demonstrations

### Multiple Means of Action and Expression:

- Drawing, speaking, folding, cutting, pointing as valid responses
- Written and oral explanations both honored
- Performance assessments complement paper tasks

## SMALL-GROUP INTERVENTION PROTOCOLS

When a student or group of students needs more support, organize **Small-Group Interventions**:

### Structure:

- 3-5 students in a group
- 15-20 minutes, 3x/week
- Pulled aside during independent work time
- Teacher-led (or trained aide)

### Content:

- Re-teach the most foundational concept
- Use concrete materials exclusively
- Slow pace, many repetitions
- Celebrate each small gain

### Duration:

- 2-4 weeks for most interventions
- Re-assess after 2 weeks; adjust

### Exit criteria:

- Student demonstrates target concept at Meeting level on 3 consecutive checks

## COMMUNICATION WITH FAMILIES

Differentiation is transparent with families:

- **Don't hide** accommodations
- **Don't stigmatize** children receiving supports
- **DO explain** to families what supports are in place and why
- **DO invite** family input — they know their child
