# STORY SCRIPTS — READ-ALOUD TEXTS FOR THE HALVING KINGDOM

> *Scripts for key story moments throughout the year. Teachers read aloud, adapt voices, and invite participation.*

---

## HOW TO USE THESE SCRIPTS

- Read with expression — use different voices for characters
- Pause at "PIA TURNS TO YOU" moments — genuinely look at the class
- Invite participation when scripts indicate
- Feel free to improvise and personalize
- Short stories (3-7 minutes) — don't drag

---

## WEEK 1 — "PIA MEETS WHOLA"

*Reading time: 5 minutes*

Once upon a fraction, in a kingdom shaped like a perfect circle, there lived a little chef named **PIA**. Pia was six years old — exactly your age! She wore a tiny chef's apron, covered in flour smudges, and she carried a wooden spoon everywhere she went.

Pia was an APPRENTICE. That means she was learning. And today — today was the very first day of her very first job.

Her teacher was the GREAT **WHOLA THE WISE**. Whola was the keeper of the Whole Hearth — the biggest oven in the whole kingdom. Everyone in the Halving Kingdom got their food from the Whole Hearth.

Pia walked up to the Hearth. It was warm. It was golden. It was GLOWING.

"Welcome, young Pia," said Whola, smiling. "Today is your first day. Before you can feed the kingdom, I must teach you the most important lesson of all."

Pia waited.

Whola picked up a PIZZA — a big, round, whole pizza, steaming hot from the oven.

"This," said Whola, "is a WHOLE."

Pia looked at it. "A whole what?"

"Just a WHOLE," said Whola. "It is ONE. It is COMPLETE. It is UNBROKEN. Before we can share it, before we can give pieces to our friends, we must know and love the WHOLE."

Pia took the pizza in her hands. It was warm. It was big. It was ONE complete thing.

"Say it with me, Pia. WHOLE."

"Whole," said Pia.

"WHOLE," said Whola again.

And in that moment, Pia understood her first lesson. Before anything else, you must know the WHOLE.

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

And then Pia looked up. She looked right at YOU. And she said —

"Can you help me learn? This week, will you help me find WHOLES — everywhere, in every shape, in every place? Will you be my helper?"

**[PAUSE. LET CHILDREN RESPOND.]**

Yes? Oh, wonderful. Then our journey begins.

— *End of Chapter One*

## WEEK 2 — "CRUMB'S FIRST VISIT"

*Reading time: 4 minutes*

Pia had been learning about WHOLES all week. She had whole pizzas, whole apples, whole books, whole everything. She was getting VERY good at it.

But today, something new happened.

A tiny, round, fluffy creature came bouncing up to the Whole Hearth. He had big eyes, slightly crossed, and he was carrying something in his tiny paws.

"HELLO!" he said. "I'm CRUMB! I'm new here! I love fractions! LOOK WHAT I MADE!"

Crumb held up a pizza. Well — TWO pieces of a pizza.

But one piece was HUGE — almost the whole pizza.

And the other piece was TEENY. Like a crumb.

"LOOK!" said Crumb proudly. "I made HALVES! This one is half! And this one is also half!"

Pia looked at Crumb's pieces. She tilted her head. She squinted. She looked again.

"Uhhhh, Crumb?" said Pia. "I don't think those are the same size."

"WHAT?" said Crumb. "They're BOTH pizza! They both came from the SAME pizza! How can they not be halves?"

Pia didn't know what to say. She looked at the pieces. One was big. One was small. They didn't look equal.

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

Pia looked up at YOU. "Friend," she said, "something is wrong with Crumb's pieces. But I don't know what the RULE is. What do you think? When is something a HALF and when is it NOT?"

**[PAUSE. LET CHILDREN SHARE.]**

Ahhh. You see, Crumb didn't know the rule yet. But now WE do.

Halves must be EQUAL.

This week, we're going to become EQUAL-DETECTIVES. We will help Crumb. We will help Pia. And we will save the Halving Kingdom from confusing pieces.

Ready?

— *End of Chapter Two*

## WEEK 3 — "PIA'S FIRST HALVING"

*Reading time: 5 minutes*

It was the third week at the Whole Hearth. Pia had learned about wholes. Pia had learned about equal and unequal. She was ready for something new.

Whola came to her. "Pia," she said, "today you must feed two friends. They are waiting outside."

Pia opened the door.

Two figures stood there, holding hands. They looked almost exactly the same — except one was wearing a shirt that was blue on the LEFT and yellow on the RIGHT. The other wore the SAME shirt, but reversed — yellow on the left, blue on the right.

"We are HALFIE and HALVO!" they said together. "We are TWINS. We are the Halving Hills Twins! We are so hungry!"

Pia ran inside. She had ONE pizza. ONE whole pizza. She stared at it.

One pizza. Two friends.

She looked at Halfie. She looked at Halvo. She looked at the pizza.

"Uh," said Pia. "I only have ONE pizza."

"THAT'S OKAY!" said Halfie.

"We always get the SAME!" said Halvo.

"That's our RULE!" said Halfie.

"Equal or nothing!" said Halvo.

Pia was confused. How do you share ONE pizza between TWO friends? If she gives it all to Halfie, Halvo gets nothing. If she gives it all to Halvo, Halfie gets nothing. If she gives each of them their own pizza — she only HAS one!

Pia sat down. She thought. She thought hard.

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

"Friend," she said, looking right at you, "I have ONE pizza and TWO friends. I need to share it. But how? Can you help me figure this out?"

**[PAUSE. LET CHILDREN PROBLEM-SOLVE.]**

Maybe — maybe if she CUT it? Cut it right down the MIDDLE? Would that work?

Pia looked at her pizza. She imagined a line down the middle. If she cut along that line, she'd have TWO pieces. If the pieces were EQUAL, Halfie and Halvo would both be happy.

She took a deep breath. She tried.

She folded the pizza in half — edges matching, fold firm. Then, carefully, she cut along the fold.

Two pieces. EQUAL. Two HALVES.

"HALVES!" said Halfie.

"EQUAL!" said Halvo.

"HALVES!" cried Pia.

This week, WE are going to learn what Pia just learned. We are going to HALVE. With our bodies, with our hands, with paper and care. Are you ready?

— *End of Chapter Three*

## WEEK 6 — "THE FOURTH FRIENDS ARRIVE"

*Reading time: 5 minutes*

Pia was now very good at halving. She had practiced for weeks. Halfie and Halvo had visited many times, always hungry, always getting equal halves. Everyone was happy.

But one day, something NEW happened.

Pia was baking at the Whole Hearth when she heard — FOUR different voices outside.

"MY TURN!" said one voice.

"CAREFUL CAREFUL!" said another.

"THIS IS FUN!" said a third.

"Is everyone okay?" said a fourth.

Pia opened the door. Four children stood there — four of them! They were the SAME AGE — quadruplets! — but they looked DIFFERENT. One had glasses. One had curly hair. One was a little taller. One had freckles.

They stood in a line, and they introduced themselves:

"I'm FEE!"
"I'm FI!"
"I'm FO!"
"I'm FUM!"

"We're the FOURTH FRIENDS! We travel together! We share EVERYTHING!"

Pia ran to get her pizza. She had ONE whole pizza.

One pizza. FOUR friends.

She knew how to halve. But halves would only feed two. What about the other two?

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

"Hmm," said Pia, looking at you. "If I halve it, only two friends eat. What if... what if I HALVE... the HALVES?"

She tried. She folded her pizza in half. Then she folded it in half AGAIN.

She opened it up. FOUR equal pieces. FOUR FOURTHS.

"FOURTHS!" cried Fee.
"FOURTHS!" cried Fi.
"FOURTHS!" cried Fo.
"FOURTHS!" cried Fum.

"We each get a FOURTH! One-fourth each!"

Pia stared. Fee had glasses. Fum did not. Fi was taller. Fo had freckles. They ALL looked DIFFERENT. But their pizza pieces were EQUAL.

"Equal doesn't mean identical," said Fee. "Equal means SAME AMOUNT."

"Our PIECES are equal," said Fum. "We are different."

This week, we celebrate the FOURTH FRIENDS. We are going to learn about FOURTHS.

And on Friday, QUEEN EQUALLIA herself will come to our class. She will watch us. She will TEST us. And if we succeed — we will be called HALVERS AND FOURTHERS of the Halving Kingdom.

Are you ready?

— *End of Chapter Six — End of Phase 1*

## WEEK 7 — "THE SHADOW ARRIVES"

*Reading time: 5 minutes*

Phase One was finished. Queen Equallia had crowned Pia — and all of you — as Halvers and Fourthers. Everyone was proud. The kingdom was peaceful.

But not everything was well.

Far away, beyond the kingdom's edge, a shadow stirred.

His name was LORD NOTENOUGH.

Lord Notenough hated sharing. He hated equality. He wanted the kingdom to FIGHT, to FIGHT over pieces, to GIVE UP on fair sharing.

And so, one night, he sent messengers to the kingdom. They were sneaky. They wore dark cloaks. They carried bags full of PIECES.

"Here you go!" they said. "Halves! Fourths! For free!"

And they gave out pieces to the kingdom citizens.

But the pieces were FAKES.

Some LOOKED like halves — but the pieces weren't equal.

Some LOOKED like fourths — but they were actually divided into five or six or only three pieces.

The citizens didn't notice at first. They took the pieces. They tried to share them. And FIGHTS started. "This is bigger than mine!" "I got less than you!" "This isn't fair!"

The kingdom was in trouble.

Queen Equallia summoned Pia.

"Pia," she said, "we need you. You are a Halver. You are a Fourther. Now — you must become something MORE. You must become... a DETECTIVE."

"A detective?" said Pia.

"A FRACTION DETECTIVE. You must look at every piece and tell us: is it TRULY a half? Is it TRULY a fourth? Or is it a FAKE from Lord Notenough?"

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

"I can't do this alone," said Pia, looking right at you. "I need you. Will you be a Detective too? Will you help me save the kingdom?"

**[PAUSE.]**

Yes? Oh, WONDERFUL. This week, we begin our detective training. Get out your magnifying glasses. Get out your thinking caps. Lord Notenough is coming — and WE are ready.

— *End of Chapter Seven*

## WEEK 21 — "WHOLA'S CHARGE"

*Reading time: 5 minutes*

Months had passed. Pia and her helpers (that's YOU) had become skilled Detectives. They had defeated Lord Notenough's tricks in the Halving Detective Trial. The kingdom was safer.

But that winter, something worrying happened.

Whola the Wise — the grandmother-baker, the keeper of the Whole Hearth — grew tired.

She sat down more often. Her voice was softer. She slept during the afternoon.

One day, she called Pia to her.

"Pia," she said, "I must tell you something. The HUNGER STORM is coming."

"The Hunger Storm?" said Pia.

"A great storm approaches our kingdom. It will bring hunger, scarcity, and need. Only EQUAL SHARING can save us. Only fairness."

Pia was scared. "What do I do?"

Whola smiled. "You must learn to share EVERYTHING. Not just pizzas. Not just pies. But TIME. And MONEY. And LENGTH. And FRIENDSHIP. Everything in our world can be shared — and you must know how."

She handed Pia a scroll.

"This is the STORY SCROLL. Every day, it will reveal a new kingdom story. You must listen. You must find the SHARING in each story. You must solve it."

Pia took the scroll. It glowed gold.

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU]**

"I can't do this alone," said Pia, looking at you. "I need you by my side. Every day this week, and for many weeks to come, we will solve kingdom stories. Together, we will learn to share EVERYTHING. Will you help?"

**[PAUSE.]**

Yes? Then let's open the scroll. Let's hear the first story.

And the scroll began...

— *End of Chapter 21*

## WEEK 32 — "PIA'S FAREWELL"

*Reading time: 7 minutes*

The Hunger Storm had come. Dark clouds had gathered. The kingdom had feared.

But the Great Kingdom Feast had saved everyone. Through your work, through Pia's work, through the kingdom's work — every citizen had received an equal share. Nobody went hungry. The storm had retreated.

And now — now the sun shone again over the Halving Kingdom.

Queen Equallia stood at the Equal Lake. She wore her brightest robes. Every citizen of the kingdom had gathered. Families were there. Friends were there. The lake shimmered with truth-water.

One by one, she called the chefs to her.

"And now," she said, "I call Pia."

Pia walked forward. She was older now. She had grown. She stood proud in her apron, with her wooden spoon.

"Pia," said Queen Equallia, "for one year, you have served the kingdom. You have halved. You have fourthed. You have solved. You have detected. You have shared. And today — today, I name you —"

She lifted a crown. Not a small crown. A REAL crown.

"— ROYAL SHAREMASTER of the Halving Kingdom."

The kingdom cheered. Pia knelt. The crown was placed on her head. She rose — Royal Sharemaster.

And then — then she turned.

She looked at all of us. She looked at each child. She looked at YOU.

**[PIA TURNS TO YOU — the final time.]**

"My friends," she said. "I did not do this alone. I did this with YOU. Every halving. Every fourthing. Every detective moment. Every story we solved. YOU were with me. YOU helped me become who I am today. Without you, there would be no Royal Sharemaster."

She smiled.

"And so, as my first act — I name you Junior Sharemasters. Every one of you. Come forward. Receive your medal."

**[EACH CHILD APPROACHES. TEACHER AS PIA/QUEEN PLACES MEDAL.]**

Finally, when every child had been named, Pia stepped back. She looked at us all.

"This is not goodbye. Next year, you will meet me again. We will learn about COMPARING fractions. Which is bigger — one-half or one-fourth? We will find out TOGETHER."

She smiled.

"But for now — keep sharing fairly. Keep seeing wholes and halves and fourths in everything. Keep being KIND. Keep being FAIR. You are Junior Sharemasters. You always will be."

And then — then Pia bowed. Deep. Respectfully. To us.

And we bowed back.

The sun shone over the Halving Kingdom. The year was complete.

And every single one of us — every single one — was forever changed.

— *The End (for now)*

## NOTES FOR READING

These scripts are MEANT to be adapted. Feel free to:

- Change Pia's name to reflect your students' heritage
- Substitute foods to match your community
- Add local references (name landmarks in your area)
- Shorten or lengthen as attention spans allow

But keep:
- The emotional arc (wonder → challenge → triumph)
- The PIA TURNS TO YOU moments
- The inclusive language (they/them for Halfie/Halvo)
- The affirmation of growth over performance
