# CHARACTER GUIDE — THE HALVING KINGDOM

> _Characters are not decoration. In a Grade 1 curriculum, characters are the child's entry point to mathematical ideas. Halfie does not merely "represent" a half. Halfie IS a half, made of personality, voice, and behavior._

---

## CHARACTER DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. **Embodied math.** Every character's physical design, personality, and behavior encodes the mathematical concept they represent.
2. **Diverse without tokenism.** Characters reflect a range of skin tones, body types, and abilities without any character being defined by their identity markers.
3. **No stereotypes.** No "princess" is passive; no "wise elder" is a stereotype; no antagonist is coded as a marginalized group.
4. **Emotionally accessible.** Characters have feelings, make mistakes, forgive, grow. They model the emotional life of a learner.
5. **Voice distinguishable.** Every character has a unique speech pattern identifiable even without attribution.

## MAIN CHARACTERS

### PIA — The Apprentice Chef

**Role:** Protagonist, the child's avatar

**Age:** 6 (matches the learner)

**Visual Design:**

- Medium brown skin, curly black hair tied back in a kerchief
- Round cheeks, expressive dark eyes
- Wears a tiny chef's apron (stained with flour) over a simple cotton dress
- Carries a wooden spoon (her "wand")
- Height: kid-sized (not a tall adult chef)

**Personality:**

- Curious: asks "but why?" and "what if?" constantly
- Kind: always wants everyone to have enough
- Impatient: wants to solve problems NOW
- Honest: admits when she doesn't know
- Brave: tries even when she's scared

**Voice Sample:**

> _"Hmm. One pizza. Two friends. That doesn't seem right. Wait — maybe if I... no, that's not fair either. Ugh! Why is this so hard? ... Oh! OH! What if I cut it right down the middle? Would that work? Let's try!"_

**Character Growth:**

- Sept: Panics when faced with sharing problems
- Dec: Thinks through sharing problems methodically
- May: Teaches others how to share fairly

**Pedagogical Function:**
Pia is the **mistake-maker**. She tries wrong approaches on screen so children see that struggle is normal. She is also the **question-asker** — her questions are the child's questions. When Pia is confused, the child feels permission to also be confused.

**Cultural Adaptations:**

- English: Pia
- Spanish: Pía
- Mandarin: 小皮 (Xiǎo Pí)
- Arabic: بيا (Biya)
- Teachers may rename Pia to reflect local names.

---

### WHOLA THE WISE — The Elder Baker

**Role:** Mentor, keeper of the Whole Hearth

**Age:** Grandmotherly (60s-70s)

**Visual Design:**

- Dark brown skin, silver-white hair in a long braid
- Tall, round, comfortable-looking
- Wears a deep blue apron embroidered with circles (wholes)
- Carries a round wooden paddle for lifting bread
- A warm, wrinkled smile

**Mathematical Embodiment: THE WHOLE**

Whola is the whole. Her name contains it. She is round, complete, unified. She bakes at the Whole Hearth, where everything starts as ONE before it becomes parts.

**Personality:**

- Patient: never rushes Pia
- Wise but playful: knows the answer but lets Pia find it
- Warm: every interaction feels like a hug
- Firm about equality: will not tolerate unfair pieces

**Voice Sample:**

> _"My dear Pia. Look at this pizza — this one, whole, unbroken pizza. Before we can share it, we must love it. Because every piece we share came from this one whole. And when we are done sharing, all the pieces together — they are still this whole. Do you feel that?"_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Whola reinforces the **whole** as the foundation of fractions. Research shows that children who lose sight of the whole struggle with fraction magnitude. Whola is the curriculum's anchor.

**Signature Phrase:** _"Remember the whole, my dear."_

---

### HALFIE — The First Twin

**Role:** One of a pair; one half of a whole

**Age:** 6 (same as Pia)

**Visual Design:**

- Light tan skin, short black hair parted exactly down the middle
- Wears a shirt that is half blue, half yellow — split vertically
- Always stands on the LEFT side of any scene
- Has a mirror-twin (Halvo) who is their other half

**Mathematical Embodiment: ONE HALF**

Halfie is literally half. Clothing, hair, stance — all visually bisected. Halfie without Halvo feels incomplete and says so.

**Personality:**

- Playful and mirror-like: finishes Halvo's sentences
- Sensitive: gets sad when separated from Halvo
- Fair-minded: insists on getting EXACTLY the same as Halvo, never more

**Voice Sample:**

> _"Hi, Pia! I'm Halfie! This is my twin Halvo — we're the Halving Hills twins! We ALWAYS get the same. If I get a big piece and Halvo gets a small piece, I'll CRY. Because we're HALVES — we have to match!"_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Halfie + Halvo together demonstrate that **two equal halves make a whole**. Their insistence on equality is the curriculum's vehicle for teaching that halves must be equal-sized.

**Key Moments:**

- Week 1: Introduced to Pia, demands equal shares
- Week 6: Celebrates with Pia at the Equal Lake ceremony
- Week 20: Testifies at the Halving Detective Trial
- Week 32: Stands at Pia's coronation

---

### HALVO — The Second Twin

**Role:** The other half

**Age:** 6

**Visual Design:**

- Mirror of Halfie: hair parted opposite way, shirt colors reversed (yellow left, blue right)
- Always stands on the RIGHT side
- When Halfie and Halvo stand together, they form a visually complete "whole"

**Personality:**

- Matches Halfie: finishes their sentences
- Slightly more cautious than Halfie
- Also insists on equality

**Voice Sample:**

> _"...and we can't be split up into unequal pieces! If Halfie has more, I feel sad. If I have more, Halfie feels sad. Equal or nothing, that's our rule!"_

**Design Note:**
Halfie and Halvo are intentionally NOT gendered. The curriculum uses "they/them" pronouns for both. This avoids reinforcing gender binaries and allows all children to see themselves in either twin.

### THE FOURTH FRIENDS: Fee, Fi, Fo, Fum

**Role:** Quadruplet siblings; the four fourths of a whole

**Age:** 6 (quadruplets, same age)

**Visual Design:**

- Four siblings of similar height and build, diverse skin tones (brown, tan, dark brown, light brown) — deliberately varied to show equal-ness doesn't mean identical
- Each wears a shirt of the same style in a different color (red, blue, green, yellow)
- When standing together in a circle, they form a complete "fourths" pinwheel

**Mathematical Embodiment: ONE-FOURTH EACH / FOUR FOURTHS TOGETHER**

Four distinct individuals, each different, but each getting exactly one-fourth. Critical lesson: **equal share ≠ identical people**. Fee, Fi, Fo, and Fum look different but get the same-sized piece.

**Personality (distinct for each):**

- **Fee:** The eager one. "Me first!"
- **Fi:** The careful one. "Let's measure carefully."
- **Fo:** The joyful one. "This is FUN!"
- **Fum:** The gentle one. "Is everyone okay?"

**Voice Sample (group):**

> _"We're the Fourth Friends! We're four, so we each get ONE-FOURTH. We look different — I have glasses, Fi doesn't, Fo has freckles, Fum has curly hair — but our PIECES are all the same size. That's what equal means!"_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Core to Week 5 onward. Fee, Fi, Fo, Fum introduce fourths AND reinforce that equality in math is about QUANTITY, not identity of the people receiving. This is also a subtle anti-bias lesson.

### QUEEN EQUALLIA — The Judge of Equality

**Role:** Ruler of the Halving Kingdom; moral authority; dispenser of truth-water

**Age:** Adult (40s-50s)

**Visual Design:**

- Rich, deep purple skin (magical, not meant to match any real ethnicity — she is literally a fantasy queen)
- Tall, wearing a robe that is half silver, half gold
- Wears a crown shaped like two equal halves of a circle
- Carries the Equal Lake Pitcher filled with truth-water

**Mathematical Embodiment: THE PRINCIPLE OF EQUALITY**

Queen Equallia is the embodiment of "equal." Her truth-water glows on equal pieces and dims on unequal ones — she is a walking equality-detector.

**Personality:**

- Regal but kind
- Values fairness above all
- Honors children's attempts even when imperfect
- Firm: does not let unfair pieces pass

**Voice Sample:**

> _"Dear chef Pia. You have brought me two pieces, asking if they are equal halves. Watch as I pour the truth-water... [pours]... See how it glows gold on both sides! Yes, these are true halves. You have honored the kingdom's law."_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Queen Equallia provides **milestone feedback**. Every four weeks, she appears to validate the child's progress through a ceremonial act. This is ritualized assessment — formative, narrative, affirming.

**Design Note:**
Her purple skin is deliberately fantastical to avoid real-world racial coding. She is a "queen of a magical kingdom," not a queen of any real ethnicity. Districts wanting a non-monarchy variant can use "Elder Equallia" or "Judge Equallia."

## SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

### LORD NOTENOUGH — The Antagonist

**Role:** Creator of sharing problems; embodiment of scarcity and unfairness

**Age:** Ageless (shadow creature)

**Visual Design:**

- Tall, thin, made of swirling gray shadow
- Wears a ragged cloak
- Carries a jagged knife that cuts UNEQUAL pieces
- Face is obscured — he is more silhouette than person
- NEVER depicted with any identifiable ethnic, religious, or cultural coding

**Mathematical Embodiment: INEQUALITY, SCARCITY, FAKE-EQUAL**

Lord Notenough creates pieces that LOOK equal but aren't. He tempts children to accept unfair divisions. He is the source of all fraction misconceptions made malicious.

**Personality:**

- Sneaky, never violent (appropriate for 6-year-olds)
- Tries to trick Pia, not hurt her
- Defeated by careful inspection, never by force
- NEVER threatening in a way that would scare children

**Voice Sample:**

> _"Oh, Pia, look at these lovely pieces I made for you! They are HALVES — I promise! [They are clearly unequal.] Just give one to Halfie and one to Halvo and no one will notice... right?"_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Lord Notenough is the "antagonist" who makes fractions MATTER. Without someone creating fake pieces, there would be no need to detect fake pieces. He transforms equality-checking from a chore into a heroic act.

**Design Guardrails:**

- NEVER depicted as a specific ethnicity, religion, or culture
- NEVER physically threatening (no weapons pointed at characters)
- ALWAYS defeated by cleverness, not violence
- His name is comical ("Notenough") to reduce fear
- Parents and teachers are encouraged to treat him as a "silly trickster" in discussion

### CRUMB THE CONFUSED — The Misconception Character

**Role:** Lovable misconception generator; friend to Pia

**Age:** 5 (younger than Pia)

**Visual Design:**

- Small, round, fluffy creature (looks like a blob with legs)
- Color: warm tan, like a cookie
- Big eyes, slightly crossed
- Always carrying a tiny, usually-wrong fraction piece
- Adorable, NEVER pitiable

**Mathematical Embodiment: COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS**

Crumb is the curriculum's wrong-example generator. He shows up with:

- Pieces that are unequal but he calls "halves"
- Too many pieces (six pieces of a pizza) and calls them "fourths"
- Pieces that overlap
- Pieces from different pizzas combined into a "whole"

**Personality:**

- Enthusiastic: always excited to share his work
- Good-hearted: wants to do math right, just gets confused
- Grateful: happy when corrected
- Funny: his mistakes are played for gentle humor, never mockery

**Voice Sample:**

> _"LOOK, Pia! I made halves! See? This is half [holds up tiny piece] and this is also half [holds up huge piece]! Aren't they great? What do you mean they're not equal? They BOTH came from the pizza!"_

**Pedagogical Function:**
Crumb is the **misconception confrontation mechanism**. Research (Bransford et al., 2000) shows that students learn misconceptions best when they explicitly confront and correct them. Crumb makes this safe and fun — the child corrects Crumb, not themselves, so no shame is attached.

**Critical Design Principle:**
Crumb is NEVER a "bad student." He is NEVER mocked. He is NEVER excluded. The class LOVES Crumb and gently teaches him. This models how classrooms should handle confusion — with love, not shame.

### THE FRACTION FAIRY — Reward Giver

**Role:** Appears when children achieve mastery; provides magical celebrations

**Age:** Timeless

**Visual Design:**

- Small, sparkly, ambiguously-gendered
- Dress/cloak is made of moving fraction shapes (halves and fourths)
- Translucent wings
- Carries a tiny wand

**Mathematical Embodiment: CELEBRATION OF MASTERY**

The Fraction Fairy is not about math content — she's about the **joy** of doing math well.

**Voice Sample:**

> *"Oooooh! Look what you did! You shared that pizza in PERFECT halves! Let me sprinkle you with fraction dust! *sparkle\* _sparkle_ _sparkle_ Now you are a CERTIFIED Halver!"\*

**Pedagogical Function:**
Provides intrinsic-feeling rewards (celebration, recognition) rather than extrinsic ones (stickers, grades). Research on motivation (Deci & Ryan, 1985) supports celebration-based reinforcement over tangible rewards for deep learning.

## MINOR / RECURRING CHARACTERS

### The Fold Folk

Tiny people who live in the creases of folded paper. They appear in folding activities (Week 7+) and celebrate when folds are straight and equal.

### The Equal Lake Fishes

Silver fish in the Equal Lake whose scales glow when they swim past equal pieces. Used in digital activities for visual feedback.

### The Hunger Storm Clouds

Not characters but visual presence — dark clouds that gather when sharing fails in Act III. They dissipate when sharing succeeds.

### Scribe Scroll

A talking scroll who records the kingdom's fraction laws. Used in read-aloud to introduce vocabulary formally.

## VOICE AND DIALOGUE CRAFT

Each character should be identifiable from a line of dialogue alone. Writers must:

1. **Vary sentence length.** Pia uses short, exclamatory sentences. Whola uses longer, flowing sentences.
2. **Use signature phrases.** "Remember the whole, my dear." (Whola). "Equal or nothing!" (Halfie/Halvo).
3. **Match vocabulary to character.** Queen Equallia uses formal language; Crumb uses tumbled, excited language.
4. **Avoid character drift.** If writers are adapting read-alouds, use this guide to check voice.

---

## INCLUSIVITY AUDIT

The character roster has been designed to:

✅ Include diverse skin tones across main characters (Pia: brown; Whola: dark brown; Fee/Fi/Fo/Fum: varied)
✅ Avoid binary gender where possible (Halfie/Halvo use they/them)
✅ Include a range of body types (Whola is round; Fee/Fi/Fo/Fum vary)
✅ Include visible accessibility (one of the Fourth Friends wears glasses; Fum uses gentle voice suggesting introversion is valued)
✅ Avoid any antagonist coded to real-world group
✅ Honor that "equal" means equal quantity, not identical people (Fourth Friends)
✅ Allow localization of names and settings

## FOR WRITERS

When creating new content involving these characters:

1. **Read their personality section before writing dialogue.**
2. **Ensure character serves the mathematical function.**
3. **Pass the Four Tests** (Child, Math, Equity, Transfer).
4. **Submit to peer review** before inclusion in curriculum.
