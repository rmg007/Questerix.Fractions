# ACTIVITY 02 — EQUAL OR NOT

> *"Before a child can make equal pieces, they must see inequality. The eye must learn to judge before the hand can act."*

**Phase:** Discovery | **Week:** 2 | **Duration:** 5 days (30-40 min/day) | **Standard:** CCSS 1.G.A.3 (foundational)

---

## ACTIVITY OVERVIEW

**Goal:** Develop visual and reasoning skills for judging EQUAL vs. UNEQUAL divisions — BEFORE teaching children to create equal divisions themselves.

**Core Insight:**
The word "half" in CCSS 1.G.A.3 is not just any two pieces — it is two EQUAL pieces. Most Grade 1 curricula teach "cutting in half" before children can reliably recognize equal-ness. This is backwards. You cannot produce what you cannot see. This week, we build the **perceptual and reasoning foundation** for equality, using Crumb the Confused as our teaching partner.

**Learning Objectives:**
- **D2:** Students can distinguish equal from unequal divisions in multiple visual contexts.
- **D3:** Students can articulate WHY two pieces are or are not equal.
- **D4:** Students begin to use the language "equal," "same," "fair," "matching."

---

## PEDAGOGICAL ANCHORS

- **Perception before production:** Visual judgment precedes motor production (Gibson, 1969).
- **Misconception confrontation:** Crumb's wrong examples teach through contrast (Bransford et al., 2000).
- **Embodied cognition:** Children use their bodies to "be" equal (matching poses, equal steps).
- **Vygotsky's ZPD:** Teacher scaffolds reasoning with guiding questions.

## FIVE-DAY PLAN

### DAY 1 — MEET CRUMB AND DISCOVER INEQUALITY

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Materials:**
- 1 paper pizza (pre-made from Week 1) per child
- Read-aloud: "Crumb's First Visit" (in `narrative/STORY_SCRIPTS.md`)
- Pairs of object pictures (some equal, some unequal) on slides or a flipbook

**Story (5 min):**

> *"Remember Pia? Yesterday Pia was at the Whole Hearth, and someone NEW came to visit. His name is Crumb. Crumb is SO excited about fractions. He wants to share things. But Crumb is a little... confused. Let me read you what happened."*

[Read "Crumb's First Visit"]

The story's punchline: Crumb tears his pizza into two very unequal pieces and proudly calls them "halves." Pia is confused. "Those aren't the same size!" Crumb says, "But they're both pizza!"

**Activity: The Equal-or-Not Game (20 min):**

Teacher projects or holds up pairs of pictures:
- Two identical cookies → children show a BIG THUMBS UP ("equal!")
- One big cookie and one tiny cookie → children show a BIG THUMBS DOWN ("not equal!")
- Two pizza pieces, same size → thumbs up
- Two pizza pieces, clearly different sizes → thumbs down
- Two glasses with same water level → thumbs up
- Two glasses, different water levels → thumbs down
- Two stacks of 3 blocks each → thumbs up
- A stack of 3 and a stack of 7 → thumbs down

For each, the teacher asks one or two children: *"How do you KNOW they're equal/not equal?"*

Children develop and articulate rules:
- "Same size."
- "They match."
- "If you put them on top of each other, they look the same."
- "You couldn't tell which one is which."

**Closure (5 min):**

> *"Today we became EQUAL-DETECTIVES. Tomorrow, we'll help Crumb fix his mistakes."*

---

### DAY 2 — FIXING CRUMB'S PIZZAS

**Duration:** 35 minutes

**Materials:**
- "Crumb's Mistake Cards" — a deck of 10 cards, each showing a pizza divided into 2 pieces
  - 5 are TRULY halved (equal pieces)
  - 5 are CRUMB-halved (unequal pieces)
- Glue, scissors (teacher-cut only if needed), correction markers

**Opening (10 min):**

> *"Crumb brought us 10 pizzas. He says they are all halved. But Pia is suspicious. Let's check Crumb's work!"*

Teacher reveals the cards one at a time. Children in pairs discuss: "Real halves or Crumb halves?"

**Work Session (20 min):**

Each pair receives 5 Mistake Cards. For each:
1. Decide: real half or Crumb half?
2. If Crumb half, DRAW the correct line on the card.
3. Write (or draw) WHY the original was wrong.

Teacher circulates with prompts:
- *"How can you tell?"*
- *"If you traced the line, would the two pieces match?"*
- *"What would make this REALLY equal?"*

**Gathering (5 min):**

Pairs share one card each. Class votes on whether the correction is right.

**Closure:**
> *"Crumb is getting better because WE are teaching him. Great detectives!"*

---

### DAY 3 — BODY-EQUALITY

**Duration:** 35 minutes

**Materials:**
- Open floor space
- Two matching mats (yoga mats or towels)
- Music
- Measuring tape (for teacher demonstration)

**Purpose:**
Embodied learning. Children use their BODIES to understand "equal."

**Opening (5 min):**

> *"Today we're going to BE equal. Not just look at equal — BECOME equal. Everyone find a partner."*

**Body Activities (25 min):**

**1. The Mirror Game (5 min):**
Partners face each other. One is the "leader," one is the "mirror." Leader moves slowly; mirror copies exactly. After 2 minutes, switch. Debrief: *"Were you equal? How could you tell?"*

**2. Equal Steps (5 min):**
Pairs line up at a wall. Both partners try to take the SAME number of steps to reach the opposite wall. Did they arrive together? If not, they took unequal steps. Try again.

**3. Equal Towers (5 min):**
Each pair gets 20 blocks. They build two towers, trying to make them EXACTLY equal in height. Teacher measures with tape: were they truly equal? Discuss.

**4. The Balance Test (10 min):**
In pairs, one partner holds out both arms. The other places objects (paper cups, light blocks) in each hand. They try to make the arms balance equally. The body itself becomes a balance scale. Children feel when their arms are NOT equal.

**Gathering (5 min):**

> *"Your body can TELL when things are equal. That's why we feel it's unfair when someone gets more than us. Our body KNOWS equal."*

This connects math to the child's lived sense of justice — powerful motivation.

**Closure:**
> *"Tomorrow we'll look at equal pieces in many different ways."*

---

### DAY 4 — EQUAL IN MANY FORMS

**Duration:** 40 minutes

**Materials:**
- "Equal Gallery" sorting sheets with many pairs of pictures
- Stickers or markers for sorting
- Tray with actual objects for comparison

**Purpose:**
Broaden the concept of "equal" across contexts. Equal isn't just "same size" — it's "same amount" in the relevant dimension.

**Opening (5 min):**

> *"Equal can mean many things. Watch this."*

Teacher holds up two different-shaped glasses with same water amount.
> *"Are these equal?"*

Children often say no because the glasses are different. Teacher pours water from one glass into a matching glass of the other shape. Water levels match.

> *"OHHHH! They had the SAME WATER, even though the glasses looked different! Equal can be tricky."*

**Sorting Activity (25 min):**

Children work with a partner on the Equal Gallery Sheet. It contains many paired pictures. They sort into "Equal" and "Not Equal" categories:

- Two apples same size (equal)
- Two different apples (not equal)
- Two containers, same height but one is fuller (not equal)
- Two lines, same length but different thickness (equal in length, which is what we're judging)
- Two groups of coins, same count different arrangement (equal)
- Two groups of coins, different counts (not equal)
- Two cakes, same size, different decorations (equal in size)
- Two rectangles, one vertical one horizontal, same area (equal — challenging!)

The last item is critical: two halves CAN look different and still be equal. This prepares for later fraction work.

**Teacher-Led Discussion (10 min):**

Show the rectangle pair (one vertical, one horizontal, same area).
> *"These are the same SIZE but DIFFERENT SHAPES on the page. Are they equal?"*

This generates debate. Teacher guides:
> *"Equal can mean 'takes up the same amount of space.' It doesn't have to look the same. Halfie and Halvo stand different ways, but they are equal halves."*

This is a critical insight for future activities (halves of different-shaped regions).

**Closure:**
> *"Equal can LOOK different. But it always means SAME amount. Tomorrow, we bring this all together."*

---

### DAY 5 — EQUALITY ASSESSMENT AND CELEBRATION

**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Materials:**
- Equality Assessment Sheet (provided, `assessment/02_Equality_Assessment.pdf`)
- Crumb stuffie or puppet (optional)
- "Equal Detective" badges (printable, `materials/02_Detective_Badges.pdf`)

**Opening (5 min):**

> *"Today we finish our week as Equal-Detectives. And guess who's coming? CRUMB!"*

Teacher (using Crumb puppet or character voice) "arrives":
> *"Hi friends! I brought 8 pictures. Please tell me which ones are equal. I still get SO confused. Help me!"*

**Assessment (25 min):**

Children work individually on the Equality Assessment Sheet:

**Part 1: Circle the Equal Pairs.** 6 pairs of pictures. Child circles only the pairs that are equal.

**Part 2: Fix It.** 3 pictures of unequal pairs. Child redraws one side to make it equal.

**Part 3: Explain.** 1-on-1 with teacher: "How do you know if two things are equal?"

Teacher assesses:
- **Meeting:** 5+ correct on Part 1, meaningful corrections on Part 2, coherent explanation on Part 3.
- **Developing:** 3-4 correct, partial corrections, emerging explanation.
- **Beginning:** Under 3 correct — needs revisit and small-group intervention.

**Celebration (15 min):**

Teacher "pins" Equal Detective badges on each child (sticker or printable pin). Class chants: "We are detectives! We see equal! We see unequal!"

Crumb thanks the class:
> *"You helped me so much. I'm going to keep learning. I'll see you soon!"*

**Closure:**
> *"You are now EQUAL-DETECTIVES. Next week, Pia has her first big problem — she has ONE pizza and TWO hungry friends. Can you guess what she might do?"*

Children speculate. Teacher doesn't give the answer — saves it for Week 3.

## DIFFERENTIATION

### For English Language Learners:
- Vocabulary cards with visuals: "equal" (two identical fruits), "not equal" (one big, one small)
- Chant routine: "Equal means SAME. Not equal means DIFFERENT."
- Partner with a buddy for discussion tasks.

### For Students with Disabilities:
- Enlarged pictures for visual processing
- Tactile equal/not equal objects (cotton ball in each hand — are they equal weight?)
- Oral-response options for assessment (no writing required)

### For Advanced Students:
- Extension: "Can two things be equal in one way and unequal in another way?" (e.g., same color, different size)
- Extension: Create their own Crumb Mistake Card for a classmate to fix.
- Journal: "What does 'fair' mean?"

### For Students Struggling:
- Extend to 1.5 weeks
- Use REAL objects instead of pictures
- 1-on-1: "Show me two equal things. Show me two not-equal things."
- Re-anchor: revisit Week 1's "whole" if the concept of whole is still fragile.

---

## COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS THIS WEEK

**M1: "If they look different, they can't be equal."**
*Mitigation:* Day 4 contrasts shape and amount explicitly.

**M2: "Equal means identical."**
*Mitigation:* The Fourth Friends (introduced later) look different but get equal pieces. Day 4 pre-teaches this.

**M3: "Bigger is always more."** (Size vs. quantity confusion)
*Mitigation:* Volume-across-glasses demonstration on Day 4.

**M4: "Equal is about counting."**
*Mitigation:* Depends on what we're counting. Sometimes (coins) equality is count; sometimes (pizza pieces) equality is size. We distinguish these clearly.

**M5: "Crumb is dumb."**
*Mitigation:* Teacher models affection for Crumb. "Crumb is learning, just like us. We all get confused sometimes. We help each other."
