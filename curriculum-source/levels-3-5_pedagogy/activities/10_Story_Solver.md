# ACTIVITY 10 — STORY SOLVER

> _"A word problem is not a math problem in disguise. It is a real-world problem in math's clothing. Teach children to find the fraction hiding in the story."_

**Phase:** Transfer | **Weeks:** 21-22 (2-week cluster, continues informally through Phase 3) | **Duration:** 35-45 min/day | **Standard:** CCSS 1.G.A.3, CCSS SMP.1-3

---

## ACTIVITY OVERVIEW

**Goal:** Teach children to read short word problems, identify the fractional situation, and solve with reasoning and language.

**Why This Activity:**
Phase 3 (Transfer) begins with word problems because transfer starts with recognizing that fractions EXIST in problems not framed as "fraction problems." This is the first formal word-problem block in the curriculum (informal word problems have appeared throughout).

**Learning Objectives:**

- **T6:** Solve word problems involving halving and fourthing
- **SMP.1:** Persevere through unfamiliar problems
- **SMP.2:** Reason quantitatively about shares
- **SMP.3:** Explain reasoning in words and drawings

---

## NARRATIVE FRAMING

Whola the Wise grows ill. Before she is too weak to speak, she summons Pia:

> _"The Hunger Storm approaches in twelve weeks. Only equal sharing can save the kingdom. But sharing is not always pizza. Sometimes it is time. Sometimes it is money. Sometimes it is friendship. You must learn to share EVERYTHING. Listen to the kingdom's stories. Find the sharing in each one. Solve them."_

Pia receives the **Story Scroll** — a magical scroll that reveals kingdom stories. Children, as Pia's helpers, solve one story per day.

## TWO-WEEK PLAN

### WEEK 21 — INTRODUCTION TO STORY-SOLVING

### Day 1 — Whola's Charge

**Duration:** 40 min

**Materials:**

- Read-aloud: "Whola's Charge"
- Story Scroll template
- First story to solve: "The Broken Bread"

**Story (15 min):**
Read Whola's charge. Unveil the first story:

> _"In the village of Half Harbor, a baker made ONE loaf of bread. Two hungry travelers came to the door. How much bread did each traveler get?"_

**Collaborative Solving (20 min):**

Teacher models the **Story Solver Method**:

1. **READ:** What happened?
2. **DRAW:** What does the situation look like?
3. **DECIDE:** Is this a halving or fourthing problem?
4. **SOLVE:** Do the math (draw the divided whole).
5. **EXPLAIN:** Say the answer in a sentence.

Class solves together. Answer: each traveler got one half of the loaf.

**Closure (5 min):**
Vocabulary: "word problem," "solve," "explain."

### Day 2 — The Story Solver Toolkit

**Duration:** 40 min

**Materials:**

- Story Solver Toolkit (a folder with: crayons, blank shapes, scissors, a word-list card)

**Activity:**
Each child receives a Toolkit. Teacher projects a new story:

> _"Four friends share one pizza. How much does each friend get?"_

Children use toolkit:

- Draw the pizza (circle).
- Decide: 4 friends → fourths.
- Fold or draw lines.
- Label each piece "one-fourth."
- Write/dictate answer: "Each friend gets one-fourth of the pizza."

Teacher circulates, confers with each child.

### Day 3 — Two Problems in One Day

**Duration:** 45 min

Children solve two problems at tables:

**Problem A:**

> _"Pia baked one pie. She ate half and gave half to Halfie. How much did Halfie get?"_

**Problem B:**

> _"Eight cookies. Shared among 4 friends. How many cookies each?"_

Children discuss strategies in pairs. Share out.

### Day 4 — The Tricky Story

**Duration:** 45 min

**Materials:**

- A harder story that requires thinking

**Problem:**

> _"Mira has 6 crackers. She wants to share them equally with her brother. How many crackers does each get?"_

Many children miscount. Teacher uses this to reinforce:

- 6 ÷ 2 = 3 (halving a set)
- Each gets HALF of the 6 crackers = 3 crackers.
- "Half" means a specific amount depending on the whole.

### Day 5 — Weekly Reflection

**Duration:** 40 min

Children select their favorite story from the week and present to the class: what was the problem, how they solved it, what they learned.

---

### WEEK 22 — EXPANDING STORY CONTEXTS

Each day introduces a NEW kind of sharing problem:

**Day 1 — Sharing Time**

> _"Pia practices piano for half an hour. If she practices at 2:00, when does she stop?"_

This introduces time-halving. Teacher provides clock models.

**Day 2 — Sharing Money**

> _"Pia has one dollar. She wants to share it half and half with Halfie. How much does Halfie get?"_

Introduces money-halving (50 cents = half a dollar).

**Day 3 — Sharing Length**

> _"Pia has a ribbon that is 12 inches long. She cuts it in half for Halvo. How long is Halvo's piece?"_

Length-halving with concrete objects.

**Day 4 — Sharing Friendship (a social problem)**

> _"Pia has 4 balloons. She wants to share with 3 friends equally, including herself. How many balloons each?"_

4 ÷ 4 = 1. Each gets one. But the twist: "Is this fourthing?" Yes — fourthing a set of 4.

**Day 5 — The Week's Challenge**
Combined problem requiring multiple fraction uses:

> _"Pia has a 12-inch ribbon AND 8 cookies AND one hour. She wants to share with 3 friends (plus herself — 4 people total). How much of each does each friend get?"_

Children work in teams. Present solutions.

## STORY SOLVER METHOD (POSTED IN CLASSROOM)

A printable poster:

**THE 5 STEPS OF STORY SOLVING**

1. 📖 **READ** the story.
2. ✏️ **DRAW** what you see.
3. 🤔 **DECIDE** — halve or fourth?
4. ✂️ **SOLVE** with pictures.
5. 🗣️ **EXPLAIN** your answer.

Displayed throughout Phase 3.

## DIFFERENTIATION

### For ELLs:

- Stories available in audio (listen as you read)
- Bilingual versions available
- Partner with bilingual peer for Step 1 (reading)
- Focus on Step 2 (drawing) as entry point

### For Motor Challenges:

- Pre-drawn shape templates for Step 2
- Larger manipulatives
- Dictation option for Step 5

### For Advanced:

- Write their own story problems for peers
- Challenge: "Can you invent a problem where BOTH halving AND fourthing are needed?"
- Journal: "Teach a kindergartener how to solve a story problem."

### For Struggling:

- Focus on Steps 1-3 only; teacher completes Steps 4-5 with child
- Fewer problems per day (1 instead of 2)
- Pair with a "solve buddy" — peer mentor
- Use physical manipulatives for every problem

---

## COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS ADDRESSED

**M1:** "A word problem is asking for a fraction, so the answer is a fraction."
_Mitigation:_ Sometimes the answer is a whole number (6 cookies halved = 3 cookies).

**M2:** "Halving means the answer is half."
_Mitigation:_ Halving is an ACTION. The RESULT depends on the whole. Half of 6 is 3; half of 10 is 5.

**M3:** "Word problems are scary because they have lots of words."
_Mitigation:_ The 5-Step Method demystifies. Drawing (Step 2) removes verbal burden.

## ASSESSMENT DATA

- Story Scroll portfolio (one story solved per day)
- Reasoning quality (rubric applied to explanations)
- Successful identification of halve vs. fourth
- Language use: "half of," "fourth of," "each gets"

## NARRATIVE INTEGRATION

Whola's illness is a SAD story beat. We handle it carefully:

- Whola is not dying — she is "growing weary"
- Her message to Pia is loving and hopeful
- By Week 32, Whola recovers (thanks to Pia's sharing wisdom)

This models that even sad stories can have growth and resolution — a literacy lesson alongside math.
