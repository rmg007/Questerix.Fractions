# ACTIVITY 07 — FOLDING FUN: THE FORMALIZATION BEGINS

> *"The fold is the child's first geometric proof. When two edges meet exactly, equality is demonstrated — not asserted."*

**Phase:** Formalization | **Weeks:** 7-9 (3-week cluster) | **Duration:** 30-45 min/day | **Standard:** CCSS 1.G.A.3

---

## ACTIVITY OVERVIEW

**Goal:** Develop deep, flexible folding skills that formalize halves and fourths in diverse shapes and contexts, while introducing Lord Notenough (the antagonist) and the detective stance.

**Why This Activity Cluster:**
Phase 2 begins here. The shift is from "discover" to "formalize" — children now NAME, RECOGNIZE, and DETECT fractions rather than merely producing them.

This is a **3-week cluster** (not a single week) because folding fluency is foundational. Rushing it creates fragile understanding.

**Learning Objectives (across 3 weeks):**
- **F1-F4:** Fluent halving/fourthing across many shape types
- **F8 (emerging):** Vocabulary emerging: "half," "fourth," "equal parts," "fold line"
- **F4:** Detect inequality (Lord Notenough's fake pieces)

---

## WEEK 7 — LORD NOTENOUGH'S FIRST ATTACK

### Day 1 — The Antagonist Appears

**Duration:** 45 min

**Materials:**
- Read-aloud: "The Shadow Arrives"
- Fake-piece cards (pieces that LOOK like halves/fourths but aren't)
- Real-piece cards

**Story:**
Lord Notenough appears for the first time. He sends shadow messengers with fake halves and fake fourths into the kingdom. Queen Equallia summons Pia: "You must become a Fraction Detective."

Children are inducted into the Fraction Detective Corps. They receive detective hats (paper).

**Activity:**
Children sort fake pieces from real pieces. Write in journals: "Real or fake? I know because..."

### Day 2 — Folding as Proof

**Duration:** 40 min

**Purpose:** Teach children that FOLDING proves equality.

**Activity:**
Given pairs of pieces (some real halves, some fake), children fold one on top of the other. If they match → real. If not → fake. Folding IS a proof.

### Day 3 — Rectangle Folding Variations

**Duration:** 40 min

**Activity:**
Rectangles can be halved many ways:
- Vertical fold (tall rectangle halves)
- Horizontal fold (wide rectangle halves)
- Diagonal fold (creates triangles)

Children fold all three ways and realize: all produce EQUAL halves.

**Misconception target:** Some children believe only vertical folds "count" as halving. Addressed explicitly.

### Day 4 — Square Folding Variations

**Duration:** 40 min

**Activity:**
Squares can be halved FOUR ways:
- Vertical
- Horizontal
- Two diagonal folds

Then fourthed many ways:
- Two perpendicular folds → 4 rectangles
- Two diagonal folds → 4 triangles
- Vertical + horizontal → 4 squares

Children make a "square folding mat" showing all 6 variations.

### Day 5 — Fake-Piece Detective Challenge

**Duration:** 45 min

**Activity:**
Teacher (as Lord Notenough) "sends" 20 pieces into the classroom. Children must identify real vs. fake. Each correct identification earns a Detective Point.

**Assessment:**
Sorting accuracy; reasoning quality.

---

## WEEK 8 — IRREGULAR AND TRICKY SHAPES

### Day 1-5 Overview:

Children encounter progressively tricky shapes:
- Hearts (symmetric but tricky fold)
- Stars (5-pointed — halving is surprising)
- Irregular "blobs" (can we halve them? How?)
- Long strips (length halving via folding)
- Two-dimensional objects (books, cards) to halve

**Key Insight for Week 8:**
Some shapes can be halved through folding. Others require MEASURING. Some may not be halvable at all with simple methods. Children learn that halving is a CONCEPT — the METHODS vary.

### Assessment:
Weekly sorting task: which shapes were easy? Which hard? Why?

---

## WEEK 9 — LENGTH AND SET FOLDING

### Day 1-3: Ribbon and String Halving

**Purpose:** Length halving as a specific skill.

**Activity:**
Children halve ribbons of different lengths. Use rulers (teacher-mediated) to verify.

### Day 4-5: Set Halving and Fourthing

**Activity:**
Halve sets of 8, 12, 16, 20 objects. Fourth sets of 4, 8, 12, 16. Notice: only even sets halve cleanly; only multiples of 4 fourth cleanly.

**Profound question:**
"Can we halve 7 fish?" — open discussion. Children realize: sometimes the WHOLE has to be different to halve cleanly.

## DIFFERENTIATION

### For ELLs:
- Vocabulary: "fold," "edge," "match," "fake," "real"
- Pair tasks with bilingual partners
- Gesture dictionary posted

### For Motor Challenges:
- Pre-scored folds on all paper
- Larger shapes
- Alternative: draw fold lines without folding

### For Advanced:
- Challenge: "Find a shape that CAN'T be halved with one fold."
- Investigate: regular polygons — which halve easily?

### For Struggling:
- Focus on simple shapes (circle, rectangle)
- Small-group practice sessions
- Return to Week 3-4 materials as needed

---

## COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS ADDRESSED

**M1:** "Halving means cutting down the middle vertically."
*Addressed Week 7 Day 3:* many valid directions.

**M2:** "Irregular shapes can't be halved."
*Addressed Week 8:* they can, with careful methods.

**M3:** "All sets can be halved."
*Addressed Week 9 Day 4-5:* only even sets halve cleanly into whole-object halves.
