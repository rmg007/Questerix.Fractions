## PRIMARY STANDARD: CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.1.G.A.3

**Full Text:**
> *"Partition circles and rectangles into two and four equal shares, describe the shares using the words halves, fourths, and quarters, and use the phrases half of, fourth of, and quarter of. Describe the whole as two of, or four of the shares. Understand for these examples that decomposing into more equal shares creates smaller shares."*

**Breakdown:**

| Standard Component | Curriculum Location | Evidence |
|-------------------|--------------------|----------|
| Partition circles into 2 equal shares | Activities 03, 04, 05 | Week 3 halving ceremony; Week 4 adventure |
| Partition circles into 4 equal shares | Activity 06 | Week 6 Fourths Festival |
| Partition rectangles into 2 equal shares | Activities 04, 05, 07 | Weeks 4, 5, 7-9 rectangle folding |
| Partition rectangles into 4 equal shares | Activities 06, 07 | Fourths festival, folding extensions |
| Use words "halves," "fourths," "quarters" | Activities 06, 08 | Weeks 6, 11-14 naming emphasis |
| Use phrases "half of," "fourth of," "quarter of" | Activities 08, 09 | Weeks 11-14 language activities |
| Describe whole as 2 of or 4 of shares | Activity 11 | Week 18-19 composition work |
| Understand smaller shares result from more shares | Activity 11 | Week 19-20 explicit comparison |

**Coverage Level:** Comprehensive and exceeds standard.

## STANDARDS FOR MATHEMATICAL PRACTICE (SMPs)

The Common Core includes eight mathematical practice standards that should be woven throughout instruction. Our curriculum addresses each:

### SMP.1 — Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Every "Pia turns to you" moment asks children to make sense of Pia's problem before solving.
- Crumb's mistakes require sense-making to correct.
- Week 21+ word problems explicitly require persistence.

### SMP.2 — Reason abstractly and quantitatively

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Children move from concrete (paper pizzas) to representational (drawings) to abstract (words like "half") across the year.
- Activity 11 (Build a Whole) demands quantitative reasoning: "How many fourths make a half?"

### SMP.3 — Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Equal Detective activities (Week 2 onward) require children to explain WHY two pieces are or are not equal.
- Crumb correction tasks require critique.
- Halving Detective Trial (Week 20) is a formal argument-construction task.

### SMP.4 — Model with mathematics

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Word problems (Weeks 21-32) require children to model situations fractionally.
- Real-World Hunt (Week 28+) asks children to find fractions in their world.

### SMP.5 — Use appropriate tools strategically

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Children select among folding, cutting, drawing, and discussing as tools for different sharing tasks.
- Materials list (paper, play-dough, ribbon, blocks) teaches tool selection.

### SMP.6 — Attend to precision

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Queen Equallia's truth-water enforces precise equality.
- Vocabulary precision is emphasized from Week 6 onward ("half" vs. "piece").

### SMP.7 — Look for and make use of structure

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Compositional reasoning (Level 7) explicitly surfaces structural relationships.
- "A fourth is half of a half" is a structural observation.

### SMP.8 — Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning

**Curriculum evidence:**
- Across many different contexts, children see that halving always produces two equal parts. The generality is the point.

## NCTM PRINCIPLES AND STANDARDS

### Content Standards

**Number and Operations (PreK-2):**
> *"Understand numbers, ways of representing numbers, relationships among numbers, and number systems... develop a sense of whole numbers and represent and use them in flexible ways, including relating, composing, and decomposing numbers."*

**Curriculum alignment:** Grade 1 fractions extend children's number sense into fractional quantities, maintaining NCTM's emphasis on flexibility, composition, and decomposition.

**Geometry (PreK-2):**
> *"Recognize, name, build, draw, compare, and sort two- and three-dimensional shapes."*

**Curriculum alignment:** Activities consistently involve multiple shape types and spatial reasoning.

### Process Standards

**Problem Solving, Reasoning and Proof, Communication, Connections, Representation** — all addressed through:

- Story-based problem solving (Pia's problems)
- Equal-detector reasoning
- Partner and group communication
- Real-world connections (Phase 3)
- Multiple representations: body, paper, drawing, language

## NATIONAL RESEARCH COUNCIL RECOMMENDATIONS

### From *Adding It Up* (NRC, 2001) — Five Strands of Mathematical Proficiency:

1. **Conceptual understanding** — addressed via the whole-referent foundation and compositional reasoning
2. **Procedural fluency** — addressed via repeated practice with folding, naming, recognizing
3. **Strategic competence** — addressed via word problems and real-world applications in Phase 3
4. **Adaptive reasoning** — addressed via Equal Detective and Halving Detective tasks
5. **Productive disposition** — addressed via identity-affirming pedagogy and narrative engagement

## STANDARDS THAT THIS CURRICULUM DOES NOT MEET (BY DESIGN)

This curriculum **intentionally does not cover**:

### Symbolic Fraction Notation (1/2, 1/4)
**Why not:** CCSS does not require it in Grade 1. Research (Tzur, 2020; Van de Walle et al., 2019) strongly supports delaying symbols until Grade 2 or Grade 3 to protect magnitude understanding.

**What we do instead:** We build verbal fluency ("one half," "one fourth"). When children transition to Grade 2, they'll learn symbols quickly BECAUSE they already know what the symbols mean.

### Comparing Fractions (1/2 vs. 1/4)
**Why not:** CCSS 1.G.A.3 includes only an implicit comparison ("more shares → smaller shares"). Formal comparison is Grade 3.

**What we do:** We introduce informal comparison in Week 19-20 through composition ("two fourths = one half") but avoid formal > and < relationships.

### Fractions Greater Than 1 (Improper Fractions)
**Why not:** Far beyond Grade 1 scope.

**What we do:** We stay firmly in the unit interval [0, 1] for the whole year.

### Equivalent Fractions
**Why not:** Formal equivalence is Grade 3.

**What we do:** We DO introduce the intuitive equivalence "two fourths = one half" in Week 19, but through physical demonstration, not symbolic manipulation.

## STANDARDS BEYOND REQUIREMENTS (INTENTIONAL ENRICHMENT)

This curriculum EXCEEDS CCSS by adding:

1. **Whole-referent explicit instruction** (Level 1) — not mandated but critical
2. **Equality recognition** (Level 2) — often skipped, but foundational
3. **Set-based fractions** (halving a set of 8) — Grade 2 in CCSS, introduced here
4. **Length/time/money halves and fourths** (Phase 3) — enrichment
5. **Compositional reasoning as explicit skill** (Level 7) — strong preparation for Grade 2-3
6. **Identity formation** (T7) — not a CCSS standard, but critical for equity outcomes
