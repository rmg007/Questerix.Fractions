# GRADE 1 FRACTION RUBRICS

> *"A rubric should help teachers SEE, not merely SCORE. Every level is a description of thinking, not a grade."*

---

## GENERAL RUBRIC PHILOSOPHY

All rubrics in this curriculum use a 4-level framework:

| Level | Descriptor | Color Code |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 4 | Exceeding | Gold |
| 3 | Meeting | Green |
| 2 | Approaching | Yellow |
| 1 | Developing | Blue |

**Color coding** is used instead of numeric letters to reduce competitive framing. A "blue" child is still learning; they are not "failing."

## CORE RUBRICS

### RUBRIC A — Whole Identification

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Identifies wholes in varied, novel contexts; articulates unprompted that a whole can be any shape or size; recognizes that a set can be a whole |
| 3 Meeting | Identifies wholes in familiar contexts; can state: "a whole is one complete thing"; distinguishes whole from partial |
| 2 Approaching | Identifies some wholes correctly but inconsistent; may confuse whole with "biggest" or "uncut" |
| 1 Developing | Limited understanding of whole as concept; may identify based on surface features only |

---

### RUBRIC B — Equal Recognition

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Identifies equal-ness in varied contexts (same-shape, different-shape, sets, lengths); can explain multiple criteria for equality; recognizes that equal pieces can look different |
| 3 Meeting | Reliably judges equal vs. unequal in familiar visual contexts; articulates "same size" or "they match" |
| 2 Approaching | Judges equality inconsistently; may confuse equal with identical |
| 1 Developing | Cannot yet reliably distinguish equal from unequal; may need manipulation to check |

---

### RUBRIC C — Halving (Action)

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Halves varied shapes in multiple ways; halves sets and lengths fluently; self-checks and corrects; uses multiple methods (fold, draw, measure) |
| 3 Meeting | Halves familiar shapes reliably; produces visually equal halves; can explain what halving means |
| 2 Approaching | Halves with some accuracy but pieces may be unequal; needs support for unfamiliar shapes |
| 1 Developing | Halving attempts produce unequal pieces; may not yet understand halving as a distinct action |

---

### RUBRIC D — Fourthing (Action)

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Fourths varied contexts fluently; understands fourth as "half of a half" AND "one of four equal parts"; generates multiple fourthing methods |
| 3 Meeting | Fourths familiar shapes reliably by halving twice; can state "4 fourths = 1 whole" |
| 2 Approaching | Fourths with support; may produce unequal parts; basic recognition |
| 1 Developing | Struggles to produce four equal parts; confuses fourths with halves |

---

### RUBRIC E — Vocabulary Use

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Uses 10+ fraction words fluently in unprompted speech; distinguishes synonyms (fourth/quarter); corrects others' incorrect language |
| 3 Meeting | Uses core vocabulary (half, fourth, equal, whole, share) accurately with prompting |
| 2 Approaching | Uses some vocabulary; may confuse terms; needs frequent scaffolding |
| 1 Developing | Limited vocabulary use; may avoid math language |

---

### RUBRIC F — Recognition in Context

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Recognizes halves and fourths in varied real-world contexts (food, time, money, length, sets); identifies unprompted during non-math time |
| 3 Meeting | Recognizes halves and fourths in familiar contexts shown during instruction |
| 2 Approaching | Recognizes in direct instruction contexts only |
| 1 Developing | Struggles to transfer recognition outside of paper-pizza examples |

---

### RUBRIC G — Compositional Reasoning

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Flexibly composes/decomposes using halves and fourths; can articulate and apply all compositional facts (2 halves=whole, 4 fourths=whole, 2 fourths=half); uses composition to solve novel problems |
| 3 Meeting | States and verifies core compositional facts; assembles pieces correctly into wholes |
| 2 Approaching | Recognizes SOME composition (e.g., 2 halves = whole) but not all; inconsistent with 2 fourths = half |
| 1 Developing | Does not yet see pieces as recomposable into wholes; treats fractions as independent |

---

### RUBRIC H — Word Problem Solving

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | Independently applies 5-step method to varied problems; solves novel contexts; explains reasoning in detail |
| 3 Meeting | Applies 5-step method to familiar problems; arrives at correct answer; offers basic explanation |
| 2 Approaching | Requires support for each step; arrives at answer with scaffolding |
| 1 Developing | Significant support needed; may not yet see problems as solvable |

---

### RUBRIC I — Mathematical Identity

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 Exceeding | States unprompted: "I am a fraction person" / "I am good at math"; shares strategies with peers; takes on leadership roles in math work |
| 3 Meeting | Expresses positive math identity when asked; participates confidently |
| 2 Approaching | Neutral math identity; participates but with some hesitation |
| 1 Developing | Expresses negative self-talk about math; may avoid participation |

**Note:** Rubric I is arguably the MOST IMPORTANT rubric. A Level 4 Mathematical Identity in Grade 1 predicts math success for years.

## TASK-SPECIFIC RUBRICS

### Whole Portfolio Review Rubric

Reviewing an entire portfolio at a milestone. Uses Rubrics A-I as subcomponents.

**Overall Levels:**
- **4 Exceeding:** 7+ of 9 rubrics at Level 4; 2 or fewer below Level 3
- **3 Meeting:** 6+ of 9 rubrics at Level 3 or higher
- **2 Approaching:** 4-5 of 9 rubrics at Level 3; others Approaching/Developing
- **1 Developing:** Majority at Level 1-2

---

### Halving Detective Trial Rubric (Week 20)

| Component | Weight | Level Expected |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| 20-Piece sorting accuracy | 40% | 15+ correct = Meeting |
| Compositional statement | 20% | Articulate "2 halves = whole" etc. = Meeting |
| Error correction | 20% | 3 of 3 corrections valid = Meeting |
| Reasoning interview | 20% | 2+ criteria articulated = Meeting |

---

### Year-End Coronation Assessment Rubric (Week 32)

| Component | Level Indicators |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Portfolio narrative | Child tells their own story with pride and accuracy |
| Transfer demo (3 domains) | Applies halves/fourths correctly in 3 different real-world contexts |
| Novel problem solve | Attempts novel problem with 5-step method; reasoning is visible |
| Identity statement | Completes "I am a fraction-knower because..." with substance |
| Year reflection | Child identifies growth areas and future interests |

## USING RUBRICS RESPONSIBLY

### Do:
- Use rubrics to understand what a child can do and what they need
- Share rubric language with children (child-friendly versions)
- Use rubrics to plan instruction, not to sort kids
- Update rubric assessments regularly as children grow

### Do Not:
- Post rubric scores publicly
- Compare children's rubric levels
- Use Level 1 as a punishment or stigma
- Treat rubrics as final judgment

---

## CHILD-FRIENDLY RUBRIC LANGUAGE

Translate rubric levels for children:

- **Gold (Exceeding):** "You can teach this to others!"
- **Green (Meeting):** "You've got it!"
- **Yellow (Approaching):** "You're on your way!"
- **Blue (Developing):** "You're growing!"

Use these child-friendly descriptors during conferences. Avoid numeric levels with children.

---

## CALIBRATION FOR TEACHERS

Teachers new to this rubric system should calibrate by:

1. Scoring 3 sample work products individually
2. Comparing with a colleague
3. Discussing discrepancies
4. Refining shared understanding

If district-wide, schedule a 2-hour calibration session at the start of the year.
