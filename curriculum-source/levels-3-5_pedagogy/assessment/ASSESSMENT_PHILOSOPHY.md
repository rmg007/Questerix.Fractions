# GRADE 1 FRACTION ASSESSMENT PHILOSOPHY

> *"Assessment is a mirror, not a gate. It tells us what the child knows so we can teach what they don't. If it does more than this — if it sorts, ranks, or labels — it has betrayed its purpose."*

---

## FOUNDATIONAL COMMITMENTS

This curriculum rejects five common assessment practices:

1. **No timed tests.** Speed is not comprehension. A Grade 1 student forced to work against a clock learns that math is about survival, not sense-making.

2. **No standardized multiple choice.** Grade 1 children cannot yet reliably demonstrate understanding through bubble sheets. The format itself introduces noise.

3. **No letter grades.** A, B, C, D designations harm young children's math identity. We use narrative descriptors: "developing," "approaching," "meeting," "exceeding."

4. **No public ranking.** No leaderboards, no "who got the most," no visible comparison charts. Every child's growth is their own.

5. **No high-stakes summative event.** The year does not culminate in a Big Test. Assessment is distributed across the year, with the portfolio as primary evidence.

---

## FIVE ASSESSMENT MODALITIES

We collect data through five complementary lenses:

### 1. OBSERVATION (Continuous)

**What:** Teacher watches children work and takes anecdotal notes.

**When:** Every day, during every activity.

**Why:** The richest data about young children comes from watching them think. A child's spontaneous comment during folding reveals more than any test.

**How:**
- Teacher carries a small notebook or clipboard
- Jots 1-2 observations per child per week
- Codes observations: ! (strength), ? (question/confusion), * (breakthrough)
- Files observations in each child's digital or paper record

**Example observation:**
> *"Maya (10/15) — folded the circle with great care, checked both halves by holding them together. Said, 'They match!' unprompted. Breakthrough on equality reasoning."*

### 2. CONFERRING (Weekly)

**What:** Brief (2-3 minute) 1-on-1 conversations between teacher and child.

**When:** At least once per child per week.

**Why:** Conferring reveals WHY a child thinks what they think. "Why" reasoning is invisible until we ask.

**How:**
- Teacher sits with child during independent work
- Asks specific questions: "Show me your half. How do you know it's a half?"
- Listens more than talks
- Records insights in the child's record

**Example conferring prompts:**
- "What did you do first?"
- "Why did you choose to fold it that way?"
- "If Halfie and Halvo saw this, would they think it's equal? Why?"
- "What's hard about this?"

### 3. PERFORMANCE TASKS (Weekly-Biweekly)

**What:** Specific, structured tasks that require the child to demonstrate a skill.

**When:** End of each activity week (5-10 per unit).

**Why:** Performance tasks surface what a child CAN DO, independently.

**How:**
- Tasks are embedded in the activity's closing day
- Not presented as "tests" — presented as "quests" or "challenges"
- Rubric-scored by teacher

**Example tasks:**
- "Halve this shape. Explain why it's equal."
- "Sort these pieces into real and fake halves."
- "Solve this word problem."

### 4. PORTFOLIO (Cumulative)

**What:** A growing collection of the child's work.

**When:** Throughout the year; assembled continuously.

**Why:** The portfolio is the DURABLE record of growth. A single piece of work tells you a moment; a portfolio tells you a trajectory.

**How:**
- Each child has a physical folder or digital portfolio
- Work added weekly
- Reviewed with child at milestones (Weeks 6, 14, 20, 27, 32)
- Shared with parents at conferences

**Portfolio Contents by Year-End:**
- Week 1: Whole Discovery portfolio
- Week 2: Equal Detective work
- Weeks 3-6: Halving and Fourths Festival materials
- Weeks 7-20: Monthly samples
- Weeks 21-32: Transfer work and coronation materials
- Final: Year reflection by child

### 5. MILESTONE CHECKPOINTS (4 per year)

**What:** Structured, ceremony-integrated assessments.

**When:** Weeks 6, 14, 20, 27, 32.

**Why:** Milestone assessments validate major phase transitions — they are pedagogically integrated, not bolted on.

**How:**
- Integrated into narrative (Queen Equallia's ceremonies, trials)
- Tasks align with phase objectives
- Results trigger differentiation decisions

**The Five Milestones:**

| Milestone | Week | Phase | What's Assessed |
|-----------|------|-------|-----------------|
| End of Phase 1 | 6 | Discovery completion | Whole, equal, halves, fourths (foundational) |
| Mid-Phase 2 | 14 | Vocabulary mastery | Naming, recognition, basic application |
| End of Phase 2 | 20 | Detective Trial | Full recognition, detection, reasoning |
| Mid-Phase 3 | 27 | Compositional | Build-a-whole reasoning |
| Year End | 32 | Coronation | Transfer, identity, holistic |

## RUBRIC FRAMEWORK

All structured tasks use a 4-level rubric:

| Level | Descriptor | What It Means |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 4 | Exceeding | Uses skill flexibly in novel contexts, explains reasoning precisely |
| 3 | Meeting | Uses skill reliably in familiar contexts, explains basic reasoning |
| 2 | Approaching | Uses skill with some support, emerging explanation |
| 1 | Developing | Needs significant support; concept still forming |

**Critical principle:** Level 1 is NEVER "failing." It is DEVELOPING. A child at Level 1 needs more time, not a lower expectation.

**Reteaching trigger:**
- If a child is at Level 1-2 on 3+ consecutive tasks, initiate small-group reteach
- If >30% of class is at Level 1-2 on a task, the ACTIVITY may need adjustment, not the children

## USE OF ASSESSMENT DATA

### For the Child:
- Conferring and portfolio review helps the child see their own growth
- Celebration of progress (not perfection) builds confidence

### For the Teacher:
- Daily observations inform tomorrow's instruction
- Weekly data informs pace adjustments
- Milestone data informs phase-level decisions

### For the Parent:
- Portfolios shared at conferences
- Narrative progress reports (not grades)
- Home-Connection letters explain what to look for

### For the School:
- Milestone data aggregated for administrative reporting
- Standards-aligned documentation available
- Privacy-respecting individual records maintained

---

## WHAT WE DO NOT USE ASSESSMENT FOR

- **Ranking students** against each other
- **Retaining students** based on fraction mastery alone
- **Tracking students** into ability-based groups
- **Public shaming** or praising
- **Teacher evaluation** based on class scores

These practices harm learning outcomes and damage math identity — the opposite of what Grade 1 needs.

---

## EQUITY IN ASSESSMENT

Our assessment system is designed to:

✅ Give every child multiple ways to demonstrate knowing (drawing, speaking, doing)
✅ Offer language accommodations for ELLs
✅ Provide physical/motor accommodations for students with disabilities
✅ Honor cultural and family sharing practices (not assume Western feast norms)
✅ Separate understanding from performance anxiety
✅ Provide repeated opportunities — not one-shot high stakes

---

## DATA STORAGE AND PRIVACY

- Digital data stored on district-approved platform
- FERPA-compliant; no public disclosure of individual scores
- Child access: portfolio goes home at year-end
- Teacher hand-off: next year's teacher receives portfolio, not numeric data alone

---

## RESEARCH BASIS

Our assessment philosophy draws from:

- **Heritage (2010)** — *Formative Assessment* — continuous, embedded data for learning
- **Wiggins & McTighe (2005)** — *Understanding by Design* — assessment serves learning goals
- **Boaler (2015)** — *Mathematical Mindsets* — growth-oriented, non-comparative assessment
- **NCTM (2014)** — *Principles to Actions* — equity-centered formative practice
- **Duckor (2017)** — formative assessment pivots
