# MASTERY CHECKPOINTS — GRADE 1 FRACTIONS

> *"Five milestones. Five opportunities for the curriculum to stop and listen. Each checkpoint is pedagogically embedded — not a test, but a celebration of what the child now knows."*

---

## THE FIVE CHECKPOINTS

| # | Name | Week | Phase | Narrative Event |
|---|------|------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | End of Phase 1 | 6 | Discovery done | Queen Equallia's First Ceremony |
| 2 | Mid-Phase 2 | 14 | Halfway through formalization | Queen's Mid-Year Review |
| 3 | End of Phase 2 | 20 | Formalization done | Halving Detective Trial |
| 4 | Mid-Phase 3 | 27 | Compositional mastery | Reunification Specialist Certification |
| 5 | Year End | 32 | Full transfer | Royal Sharemaster Coronation |

Each checkpoint has:
- Narrative framing (how it fits the story)
- Assessment tasks (what we measure)
- Decision rules (what happens based on results)
- Celebration elements (how we honor the achievement)

---

## CHECKPOINT 1 — END OF PHASE 1 (Week 6)

### Narrative Framing

Queen Equallia visits the classroom. She has heard that this class has been learning halving and fourthing. She wishes to witness their work and formally certify them.

### Assessment Tasks

**Task 1.1 — Whole Identification**
Child is shown 6 pictures (mix of wholes, partials, and ambiguous). Child circles all true wholes.
- Meeting: 5+ correct

**Task 1.2 — Equal Recognition**
Child is shown 6 pairs of pieces. Sorts into "equal" and "not equal."
- Meeting: 5+ correct

**Task 1.3 — Halving Performance**
Child is given a paper circle. Folds, marks fold line, cuts (with teacher assist if needed). Checks own work.
- Meeting: produces two visually equal halves; can explain

**Task 1.4 — Fourthing Performance**
Child is given a paper rectangle. Halves, then halves again to produce 4 equal parts.
- Meeting: produces 4 visually equal parts; can explain "half of half"

**Task 1.5 — Verbal Explanation**
Child explains (to teacher, 1-on-1): "What is a half? What is a fourth?"
- Meeting: uses accurate language, references "equal parts of a whole"

### Decision Rules

**If child meets all 5:** Proceed to Phase 2.
**If child meets 3-4:** Proceed with monitoring. Note specific task for continued support.
**If child meets 0-2:** Extend Phase 1 by 1-2 weeks with targeted small-group work. Do NOT proceed at full pace.

### Celebration Elements

- Queen Equallia (teacher or guest) pins a "HALVER & FOURTHER" badge on each child
- Class photo with Queen Equallia
- Certificates sent home
- First entry in each child's Year Portfolio

## CHECKPOINT 2 — MID-PHASE 2 (Week 14)

### Narrative Framing

Queen Equallia returns for the Mid-Year Review. The kingdom has been under increasing attack from Lord Notenough's fake pieces. She must certify which children are ready to serve as **Junior Detectives** in the upcoming Halving Detective Trial.

### Assessment Tasks

**Task 2.1 — Vocabulary Inventory**
Child uses the following words in sentences: half, halves, halved, fourth, fourths, fourthed, quarter, equal, whole, share. (Oral or written.)
- Meeting: uses 8+ correctly

**Task 2.2 — Recognition in Varied Contexts**
Child is shown 10 real-world images (pizza slices, sandwich halves, clock halves, set fractions). Names the fraction in each.
- Meeting: 7+ correct with accurate vocabulary

**Task 2.3 — Halving Across Shapes**
Child halves 3 different shapes (circle, rectangle, irregular).
- Meeting: all 3 successful, with variety of methods

**Task 2.4 — Set Fraction Task**
"Halve this set of 8 objects. Fourth this set of 12." Child completes using manipulatives.
- Meeting: correct counts (4 and 3 respectively)

**Task 2.5 — Detective Task**
10 cards shown: 5 true halves/fourths, 5 fakes. Child sorts.
- Meeting: 8+ correct identifications

### Decision Rules

**If child meets all 5:** Proceed as Junior Detective.
**If child meets 3-4:** Proceed with targeted conferring on weaker areas.
**If child meets 0-2:** Pause individual progression. Small-group intensive in Weeks 15-17 to catch up before Week 20 Detective Trial.

### Celebration Elements

- "JUNIOR DETECTIVE" certification
- Detective hat or badge upgrade
- Mid-year conference with family
- Portfolio review: "Look how much you've grown!"

---

## CHECKPOINT 3 — END OF PHASE 2: THE HALVING DETECTIVE TRIAL (Week 20)

### Narrative Framing

The kingdom holds a public trial. Lord Notenough has sent 20 pieces to the kingdom court, claiming they are all halves or fourths. The jury — our children — must judge each piece. Queen Equallia presides.

This is the NARRATIVE CLIMAX of Act II.

### Assessment Tasks

**Task 3.1 — The 20-Piece Trial**
Child receives 20 pieces (or images of pieces). Must identify:
- True halves (there are 5)
- True fourths (there are 5)
- Fake pieces that LOOK like halves/fourths but aren't (there are 10)

Child sorts and defends their judgment. Teacher can ask 2-3 follow-up questions.

- Meeting: 15+ correct identifications
- Exceeding: 18+ correct + strong verbal reasoning

**Task 3.2 — The Compositional Question**
"Two fourths together make WHAT?" Child demonstrates with paper or words.
- Meeting: "One half" or equivalent demonstration

**Task 3.3 — Error Correction**
Child is given 3 fake pieces. Redraws or corrects each to make it truly halved/fourthed.
- Meeting: all 3 corrected with valid reasoning

**Task 3.4 — Reasoning Interview**
Teacher: "Tell me how you know a piece is a real half." Child explains.
- Meeting: articulates two or more criteria ("equal parts," "both from the same whole," "they match," "if I fold them together they stack")

### Decision Rules

**If child meets all 4:** Proceed to Phase 3 fully.
**If child meets 2-3:** Proceed, but revisit Level 7 (compositional) work in early Phase 3.
**If child meets 0-1:** Significant concern. Family conference. Possible targeted intervention or recommendation for small-group work throughout Phase 3.

### Celebration Elements

- Public trial ceremony (parents invited if possible)
- "KINGDOM DETECTIVE" honor
- Large-print certificate signed by Queen Equallia
- Featured on classroom "Wall of Detectives"

## CHECKPOINT 4 — MID-PHASE 3 (Week 27)

### Narrative Framing

Reunification Specialist certification. After the 2-week Build-A-Whole cluster, children demonstrate the deepest conceptual skill: compositional reasoning.

### Assessment Tasks

**Task 4.1 — Build a Whole**
Child is given a mixed pile of halves and fourths (all from one whole). Assembles them into a complete whole.
- Meeting: successful assembly + verbal description

**Task 4.2 — Decompose and Recompose**
Child starts with a whole, decomposes to halves, further to fourths, then recomposes back to a whole.
- Meeting: full bidirectional demonstration

**Task 4.3 — Compositional Statements**
Child states three facts: "2 halves = 1 whole," "4 fourths = 1 whole," "2 fourths = 1 half." With or without demonstration.
- Meeting: all three stated accurately

**Task 4.4 — Mixed-Piece Problem**
"Build a whole using 1 half and some fourths. How many fourths do you need?" Child solves.
- Meeting: "Two fourths" + explanation

**Task 4.5 — Transfer Task**
"If four friends share a sandwich equally, what fraction does each get? If two friends share, what fraction?" Child answers.
- Meeting: "Fourth" and "half" correctly applied to sets

### Decision Rules

**If child meets 4-5:** Ready for year-end coronation.
**If child meets 2-3:** Proceed with focus on compositional language in remaining weeks.
**If child meets 0-1:** Family conference. Discuss summer learning supports.

### Celebration Elements

- "REUNIFICATION SPECIALIST" certification
- Special gold-embossed certificate
- Feature role in Hunger Storm narrative (Week 29)

---

## CHECKPOINT 5 — YEAR-END CORONATION (Week 32)

### Narrative Framing

Pia is crowned Royal Sharemaster. Each child is knighted as Junior Sharemaster. Parents attend. This is the year's emotional peak.

### Assessment Tasks

**Task 5.1 — Portfolio Review**
Teacher and child together review the Year Portfolio. Child narrates their journey: "First I learned... then I... now I can..."
- This is qualitative, not scored — but teacher notes the child's self-awareness

**Task 5.2 — Transfer Demonstration**
Child selects 3 of the 5 transfer domains (food, time, money, length, sets) and demonstrates fraction use in each.
- Meeting: 3 successful demonstrations with accurate language

**Task 5.3 — Word Problem Novel Solve**
Child solves one novel word problem they have not seen before.
- Meeting: solves with reasoning (not necessarily exact correct answer — reasoning matters)

**Task 5.4 — Identity Statement**
Child completes: "I am a fraction-knower because..."
- Qualitative — every response is honored

**Task 5.5 — Year Reflection**
"What was the hardest part? What was the most fun? What do you want to learn next year?"
- Qualitative — informs next year's teacher

### Decision Rules

**If child meets Tasks 5.2 and 5.3:** Coronation with full honors. Grade 2 ready.
**If child is emerging on 5.2 or 5.3:** Coronation with honors + detailed hand-off note to Grade 2 teacher outlining areas to monitor.
**If child significantly behind:** Coronation (no child is excluded) + detailed summer learning packet + communication with family and next teacher.

### Celebration Elements

- Coronation Ceremony (the big event)
- Royal Sharemaster Medal
- Year Portfolio as keepsake
- Commemorative class photo
- Families in attendance

**Critical principle:** EVERY CHILD IS CROWNED. The coronation is not a gated event. Every child, regardless of Level, receives the ceremony. Growth is honored; mastery gaps are addressed privately.

---

## HAND-OFF TO GRADE 2

At year-end, each child's record includes:

1. Year Portfolio (physical/digital)
2. Five Milestone reports (narrative form)
3. Summary note for Grade 2 teacher:
   - Strengths
   - Areas needing continued support
   - Specific concerns (if any)
   - Child's math identity statement

This hand-off is critical. Without it, Grade 2 teachers must re-discover what this curriculum already knows about each child.

---

## AGGREGATED DATA FOR ADMINISTRATORS

Teachers can report:
- % of class meeting each checkpoint
- Areas where class needs curriculum revision
- Identified students needing additional support

**What administrators DO NOT see:**
- Individual scores without teacher context
- Comparisons between classes
- Ranking of students

This is a firm ethical line.
