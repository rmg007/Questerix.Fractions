## Executive Summary

* **Health verdict:** 🔴 **Structurally undeveloped, observably blind, presentationally broken at first contact.** The visual layer (Fredoka typography, adventure-map composition, amber CTAs) and Dexie persistence (v3 + 17 working repositories) are the only two layers that materially work today. Everything between them — domain orchestration, learning-engine wiring, telemetry, and level-routing — is either disconnected, duplicated, or invisible.
* **Single greatest threat to long-term viability:** **The absent Application/Use-Case layer.** Phaser scenes (`Level01Scene.ts` ~1,200 LOC, `LevelScene.ts` analogous) have absorbed every cross-cutting orchestration responsibility: persistence dispatch, BKT mastery updates, misconception detection, validator routing, session-aggregate construction, level-unlock policy, hint-copy lookup, and even response-time computation. This single architectural omission is the upstream cause of (a) duplicated and divergent BKT contracts between L1 and L2+ (`skill.partition_halves` vs `skill.level_${n}`), (b) the C5 constraint breach via `localStorage` unlock state, (c) the impossibility of instrumenting validator/persistence latency (no seam exists at which to hang a `performance.now()` bracket), (d) the `await import('../persistence/...')` epidemic that hides dependencies from static analysis, and (e) the inability to unit-test gameplay logic without booting Phaser. Every other vulnerability in this report is downstream. Fixing telemetry, portability, or UX without first introducing the Application layer entrenches the rot rather than excises it.
* **Catastrophic secondary blind spot:** **Production telemetry is `/dev/null`.** `src/lib/log.ts:47` short-circuits to no-op when `import.meta.env.PROD`. The Dexie `sessionTelemetry` table has zero writers despite an active capture site at `Level01Scene.ts:333` and `LevelScene.ts:623`. No `window.onerror` handler exists. The only `unhandledrejection` listener actively *suppresses* storage errors via `event.preventDefault()`. Once a build ships to a school-managed iPad with restrictive ITP policies, every BKT corruption, validator regression, and quota eviction evaporates with no recoverable trace. This is the unknown-unknown generator that turns every Phase 2/3 fix into a guess.
* **Acute presentation defect (non-architectural, but blocks all in-browser verification):** BUG-01 (wrong archetype prompt loaded) and BUG-02 (validator never accepts a centred drag). Two-minute and thirty-minute fixes respectively. They do not change the architectural verdict but they prevent the team from verifying anything else end-to-end, and they constitute a trust-destruction event the moment a K–2 student opens the app.

## Dimension 1: Architecture & Portability

* **Primary Vulnerabilities:**
  * **Missing Application layer** (root cause of all scene bloat). No `src/application/` directory exists. Use cases (`SubmitAttemptUseCase`, `OpenSessionUseCase`, `CloseSessionUseCase`, `RecordHintUseCase`, `LoadLevelTemplatesUseCase`, `MarkLevelCompleteUseCase`, `GetUnlockedLevelsUseCase`, `ResetDeviceUseCase`, `ExportBackupUseCase`) live as inline blocks inside Phaser scene callbacks. Cited evidence: `Level01Scene.ts:277-343, 437-457, 614-686, 906-1026, 1146-1174` and `LevelScene.ts:243-260, 366-420, 596-632, 634-743`.
  * **Duplicated and divergent orchestration** between `Level01Scene` and `LevelScene` — same domain logic copied with subtle drift. `LevelScene.recordAttempt` derives `skillId` as `skill.level_${levelNumber}` (lines 666–717); `Level01Scene` writes `skill.partition_halves`. The `Session.activityId` is `partition_halves` in L1 and `level_${n}` in L2+. The BKT mastery store is silently bifurcating into two namespaces — any cross-level mastery analysis will return inconsistent results the moment Sprint 2 ships content.
  * **Constraint C5 breach** (the highest-severity correctness defect in this audit). `MenuScene.ts:338, 353-358, 365-384` reads/writes level-unlock state to `localStorage`, then merges with Dexie session results inside `_openLevelChooser()`. C5 (`docs/00-foundation/constraints.md`) explicitly forbids `localStorage` for important data; unlock progression unambiguously qualifies. Quota eviction silently destroys progress. `MenuScene.markLevelComplete` is also a `static` method called from level scenes — coupling presentation as a domain-state writer.
  * **Domain layer impurity.** `src/engine/misconceptionDetectors.ts` (lines 38, 41-42, 83, 86-87, 117, 120-121, 164, 167-168, 206, 209-210) directly invokes `crypto.randomUUID()` and `Date.now()`. `src/engine/selection.ts:87` calls `Math.random()`. `src/engine/calibration.ts:20` defines an inline `console.warn` logger to dodge the contaminated central one. `bkt.ts`'s header claims "no side effects" — siblings violate the contract; detectors are non-deterministic, not property-testable, and not replayable for forensic session reconstruction.
  * **No port interfaces above the 17 Dexie repositories** (`src/persistence/repositories/*.ts`). Every caller pulls Dexie into its dependency graph — engine, curriculum, scenes, tests. No swap path to sqlite, an HTTP-backed store, or an in-memory test fake. The architecture review treats this as solid because it is *correct for the current deployment*; under the stricter portability standard the leakage blocks any second runtime.
  * **Dynamic `await import()` epidemic.** ~18 sites in `Level01Scene.ts` (lines 187, 256, 282, 287, 297, 312, 313, 648, 654, 658, 805, 914, 915, 953, 954, 1007, 1011, 1149) and ~15 in `LevelScene.ts` defer module resolution to dodge circular-import warnings. madge/dpdm/IDE rename refactors cannot trace these edges; renames will not flag scenes as broken until runtime; bundle-splitting heuristics cannot optimize repository chunks because they appear deferred at every call site.
  * **Direct Dexie handle in presentation.** `src/scenes/SettingsScene.ts:13, 256` imports the raw `db` object and calls `db.delete()` from a UI button handler with no `ResetDeviceUseCase` audit, telemetry, or guardrail.
  * **Build-tool and host-global coupling in the logger substrate.** `src/lib/log.ts` (lines 38, 40, 47, 114, 117, 119, 120) and `src/lib/logger.ts:6` read `import.meta.env.DEV/PROD` (Vite intrinsic). `log.ts` additionally reads `window.location.search` and `localStorage`. The "centralized logger" requires Vite + a browser; this is precisely why `engine/calibration.ts` rolls its own.
  * **Composition root not assembled.** `src/main.ts` mixes Phaser bootstrap (lines 30–44), `unhandledrejection` handler (lines 7–14), and `navigator.storage.persist()` negotiation (lines 47–52) in 50 lines with no DI container. Alternate shells (Electron, Capacitor, Cloudflare Worker prerender, headless test) cannot reuse the boot sequence.
  * **Domain timestamps minted in scene code.** ~25 `Date.now()` calls across `BootScene.ts:99`, `Level01Scene.ts` (13 sites), `LevelScene.ts` (14 sites) stamp `Session.startedAt`, `Attempt.submittedAt`, `SkillMastery.lastAttemptAt`, `HintEvent.shownAt`. Scene-tier authorship of domain data; `responseMs = Date.now() - this.questionStartTime` is what flows into BKT — the engine's *inputs* are non-deterministic and use the wrong primitive (wall-clock instead of monotonic `performance.now()`).
  * **DOM coupling inside persistence.** `src/persistence/backup.ts:100-106` synthesises `<a download>` clicks (presentation concern) inside the persistence module; `src/persistence/db.ts:158-200` reads `navigator.storage`/`sessionStorage` and emits `console.warn` directly. Persistence cannot run headlessly.
  * **Embedded curriculum content in presentation.** `Level01Scene.ts:69-110` hardcodes a `QUESTIONS` array as a synthetic fallback — curriculum data leaked into a Phaser scene file.
  * **Hidden runtime dependency.** `Level01Scene.ts:313, 915` and `LevelScene.ts` analogues call `await import('nanoid').catch(...)` — and `nanoid` is **not declared** in `package.json`. Either the catch fallback always fires (silent dead path) or the build pipeline will choke once tree-shaking decides differently.

* **Required Deep-Dive:**
  1. **`src/scenes/Level01Scene.ts` and `src/scenes/LevelScene.ts`** — extract use cases; target shrink from ~1,200 to ~400 LOC each; sunset `Level01Scene.ts` once parity is reached (Decision D-4 in master plan). **Highest leverage; gates everything below.**
  2. **`src/scenes/MenuScene.ts`** — extract `ProgressionRepository` (extend the existing `progressionStat.ts`), `LevelUnlockPolicy` pure function in `src/engine/`, `GetUnlockedLevelsUseCase`, `MarkLevelCompleteUseCase`. Migrate `localStorage.unlockedLevels:*` keys on first boot; close the C5 breach.
  3. **`src/engine/`** — introduce `src/engine/ports.ts` with `Clock`, `IdGenerator`, `Rng`, `Logger`, `Viewport` interfaces; thread `DetectorContext` through every detector; inject from composition root. Add `eslint no-restricted-syntax`/`no-restricted-globals` forbidding `crypto`, `Date`, `Math.random`, `window`, `document`, `console`, `localStorage`, `fetch` in `src/engine/**` and `src/validators/**`.
  4. **`src/persistence/repositories/*` (all 17)** — define sibling `XRepository` interfaces in `src/persistence/ports/`; move concrete implementations to `src/persistence/dexie/`. Compose at boot, not at module-import time.
  5. **`src/curriculum/loader.ts`** — split into `parseCurriculumBundle(source: BundleSource)` plus `HttpBundleSource`, `EmbeddedBundleSource`, `FallbackBundleSource(primary, fallback)` adapters in `src/lib/adapters/`. Removes hardcoded `fetch('/curriculum/v1.json')` and the silent Vite JSON-import dependency at `loader.ts:26`.
  6. **`src/main.ts`** — promote to thin composition root; assemble `Composition = { clock, ids, rng, logger, bundleSource, repos, sink, permission, viewport }` and pass via `scene.data` registry.
  7. **`src/scenes/SettingsScene.ts`** — replace `db.delete()` with `ResetDeviceUseCase` (auditing, telemetry, and `lastUsedStudent.clear()` orchestration); remove the raw `db` import.

## Dimension 2: Telemetry & Observability

* **Critical Blind Spots:**
  * **Production error sink is `/dev/null`.** `src/lib/log.ts:47` returns `false` from `isEnabled` whenever `import.meta.env.PROD` is true — every `log.error(...)` and `log.warn(...)` evaporates in shipped builds. `src/lib/logger.ts:6-20` mirrors the pattern: only `console.error` survives prod, with no remote shipper. A field crash, validator exception, BKT update failure, or persistence write failure leaves no trace.
  * **No `window.onerror` handler exists.** Verified by Grep: zero matches for `window.onerror|addEventListener\s*\(\s*['"]error`. Synchronous uncaught exceptions in Phaser scene callbacks — the most common failure mode — are completely unrecoverable. The only existing `unhandledrejection` listener at `src/main.ts:7-14` actively *suppresses* storage errors via `event.preventDefault()` without persisting them.
  * **`sessionTelemetry` Dexie table is a dead-letter store.** Schema declared at `db.ts:50, 67, 80, 105, 136`; only reader is `backup.ts:61`; no writer exists in the entire `src/` tree. Capture object is constructed at `Level01Scene.ts:333` and `LevelScene.ts:623` (viewport, FPS targets) and immediately discarded — wire is cut between schema, capture, and persistence.
  * **No correlation IDs across the attempt cascade.** A single submit triggers ≥6 downstream operations (validator dispatch → `attemptRepo.add` → `runAllDetectors` → `misconceptionFlagRepo.create` × N → `bktUpdate` → `skillMasteryRepo.put`). None share a per-cascade `traceId`. Forensic reconstruction of a corrupted student record requires reasoning about wall-clock proximity in scrollback.
  * **No execution-time measurement** of (a) any of 17 Dexie repositories, (b) validator dispatch (`Level01Scene.ts:652`, `LevelScene.ts:393`), (c) curriculum bundle fetch (`loader.ts:138`), (d) seed transactions (`seed.ts`), (e) boot-phase durations (`BootScene.ts`). Verified by Grep: zero matches for `performance.now|performance.mark|performance.measure|performance.memory|PerformanceObserver`. Every latency regression is invisible. The single response-time metric in use (`responseMs = Date.now() - this.questionStartTime`) conflates user-think-time, animation time, validator time, and persistence flush time into a number that BKT then ingests as `avgResponseMs`.
  * **No structured log emission.** `log.ts:85-91` formats with `console[fn](prefix, %cStyle, %cReset, data)` — pretty in DevTools, opaque to any log aggregator. Zero JSON serialisation, zero stable schema, no level/category/timestamp/traceId tuple parseable from stdout capture.
  * **No frame-budget instrumentation.** Zero `PerformanceObserver({ entryTypes: ['longtask'] })`, no `requestAnimationFrame`-based FPS sampler, no `game.loop.actualFps` reads. Janks > 50 ms on the partition-drag path are silent on the primary K–2 device (iPad).
  * **No quota or memory telemetry.** `navigator.storage.estimate()` is called only inside `db.ts:158-200`'s grant negotiation, never to monitor headroom. `navigator.deviceMemory`, `performance.memory`, `navigator.connection.effectiveType` are unread. The very failure mode `db.ts` was written to *prevent* (`QuotaExceededError`) routes back through the suppressing `main.ts` rejection handler.
  * **Errors swallowed without any sink.** `src/lib/preferences.ts:28-34` discards exceptions with **no log call at all** — corrupt `deviceMeta` is indistinguishable from first-boot empty state. `src/scenes/MenuScene.ts:789` silently swallows font-load failures (FOUT/FOIT in production with no signal). `src/scenes/Level01Scene.ts:997-1024` reduces three nested catches to `String(err)` (drops stack), captures no originating IDs.
  * **No build/release version stamp** on any log record. Even if logs reached an aggregator, no `app.version` / `git.sha` field exists for deploy correlation.
  * **Three logger substrates competing.** `lib/log.ts` (12-channel categorical), `lib/logger.ts` (4-level wrapper), `engine/calibration.ts:20` (inline `console.warn`). Three retrofit surfaces; the engine's instinct to avoid the central logger is correct because the central one taints purity.
  * **External-fetch telemetry absent.** `loadCurriculumBundle` (`loader.ts:138`) does not record HTTP status, payload size, parse duration, or fallback frequency to the embedded `bundle.json`. A degrading CDN that pushes every user to the embedded fallback would be invisible.

* **Instrumentation Priority:** sequence is mandatory — each step removes a precondition for the next.
  1. **Step 1 (immediate, before any other code lands):** Rewrite `src/lib/log.ts` as a `Logger` factory returning `{ debug, info, warn, error: (event: string, attrs: Record<string, unknown>) => void }` with a sink array. Sinks: `ConsoleSink` (dev only), `IndexedDBSink` (always-on; writes to a new `telemetryEvents` table at Dexie schema v4 with `++id, kind, severity, ts, traceId, [traceId+ts]`). **Removes the production-silence catastrophic blind spot.** Migrate the ~5 callers of `lib/logger.ts` and the inline `engine/calibration.ts:20` logger; delete both duplicates.
  2. **Step 2 (same release):** Pair `window.addEventListener('error', ...)` with the rewritten `unhandledrejection` handler in `src/main.ts`; both route through the new `Logger.error`. Move storage-error suppression *into* the logger as a `severity: 'warn'` tag rather than dropping the record. Wire the `sessionTelemetry` writer at the end of `openSession()` in both scenes (capture viewport, `navigator.userAgent`, `navigator.deviceMemory`, `screen.devicePixelRatio`, reduced-motion snapshot). Connect the cut wire.
  3. **Step 3 (post-Application-layer extraction in Phase 2):** Wrap repositories and validators with `performance.now()` brackets at the application-service boundary (the seam that does not exist today). Emit `db.op.duration_ms`, `validator.dispatch.duration_ms`, `boot.phase.duration_ms` histograms into the IndexedDB ring buffer. Open a `tracer.startSpan('attempt.cascade')` at the top of `SubmitAttemptUseCase.execute`; propagate `spanContext().traceId` into the persisted `Attempt.traceId` (Dexie schema v5 bump).
  4. **Step 4 (release-grade):** Adopt OpenTelemetry-JS (`@opentelemetry/sdk-trace-web`, `@opentelemetry/sdk-metrics`, OTLP/HTTP exporter via `navigator.sendBeacon` on `pagehide`) and `@sentry/browser` for error aggregation. Stamp every record with `import.meta.env.VITE_GIT_SHA` injected by Vite from CI. Add `web-vitals` for INP/LCP/CLS sampling and `PerformanceObserver({ entryTypes: ['longtask'] })`.
  5. **Step 5 (privacy gate):** `DeviceMeta.preferences.telemetryConsent` honoured in the buffered exporter — IndexedDB sink always writes (in-app debugging), network flush is opt-in. Reflect in `public/privacy.html`.

## Dimension 4: UX & Cognitive Friction

The K–2 persona is, by C3, the **only** intended user for the MVP. Every interaction must clear a 5-to-7-year-old's working-memory budget. Today, several workflows demand effort the persona cannot supply.

* **Friction Points:**
  * **BUG-01 — Prompt-mechanic incoherence.** The student opens Level 1 ("Halves") and reads *"Which shape has 1/3 shaded?"* while the screen shows a draggable line on a rectangle. Three simultaneous violations of the K–2 cognitive contract: wrong archetype (`identify` vs `partition`), wrong fraction (1/3 vs 1/2), wrong action verb ("which shape" vs "split"). A 6-year-old has no recovery path. **Single highest-friction defect in the system.** Fix: filter `templatePool` to `archetype === 'partition'` in `Level01Scene` — one line, ~10 minutes including verification.
  * **BUG-02 — Validation never accepts a correct answer.** "Check ✓" produces no feedback, no progress increment, no animation. The student sits at 0/5 indefinitely with no diagnostic signal. The emotional cost of "I tried and nothing happened" on a K–2 player compounds across questions until the app is abandoned. `Level01Scene.onSubmit()` `handlePos` plumbing or snap tolerance.
  * **BUG-04 — Hint ladder stuck on Tier 1.** Repeated hint presses replay the same Tier-1 message; Tier 2 (visual midpoint overlay) and Tier 3 (worked example) never fire despite being authored. Removes the only pedagogical scaffold available to a struggling student.
  * **G-C3 — No UI route to Level 2-9.** The adventure map is decorative — exactly three tappable stations exist (Play!, Continue, Settings) per master-plan §5.1. A student who finishes L1 has nowhere to go. Direct cause of the user feedback "couldn't go till the last level." Worse: the visual affordance of a winding path with level badges *lies* about what's interactive — the child taps every node before learning none respond, exhausting working memory before reaching the actual primary action.
  * **G-C7 — "Keep going" loops Level 1.** `LevelScene.showSessionComplete()`'s "Keep going ▶" button calls `loadQuestion(questionIndex + 1)` which increments within the same level rather than advancing `levelNumber`. A student who masters halves is sent back to halves with no acknowledgement of progression — invisible regression.
  * **G-UX3 / G-UX8 — TTS built but never called.** `TTSService` (Web Speech API, K-2-tuned 0.95× rate) exists; `PreferenceToggle` works in `SettingsScene`; `tts.speak(promptText)` is **never invoked** on question load. K–2 students with developing reading fluency cannot independently start a session that requires reading the prompt. Critical accessibility regression for the target persona — turns every prompt into a literacy hurdle before it becomes a math hurdle.
  * **`SettingsScene` over-serves the K–2 persona.** 393 lines, six controls (Reduced Motion, TTS Enabled, Storage Permission, Export Backup, Reset Device, Privacy Notice). For the *actual* end user (5–7 years old), only TTS is age-relevant; the remaining five are caregiver-tier controls in a product that explicitly parks the caregiver surface (`G-UX1`) to a future milestone. The screen distracts the persona from the task and offers a one-tap path to data destruction.
  * **Settings-gear ambiguity** (BUG-05). Earlier QA flagged the gear as routing to Level 1; architecture review reclassified as IDE-preview artifact since `MenuScene` calls `scene.launch('SettingsScene')`. Until verified in a real Chrome tab, the team carries a phantom risk and cannot trust the menu's primary affordance.
  * **No K-2-emotional verification of feedback animations.** `EXACT → green + "Correct! Great work."`, `CLOSE → amber + "Almost! Try a tiny adjustment."`, `WRONG → shake + "Not quite — try again."` are blocked by BUG-02 and have never been seen by a target-age proxy. Risk: animations land flat, copy lands scary, or both.
  * **Hint copy hardcoded in scene methods** (`LevelScene.ts:495-553`) — ineligible for i18n, A/B testing, or non-engineer wording adjustment. Every copy change requires a code release.
  * **No frame-budget signal on the partition-drag path** combined with **iPad/touch untested** — the core mechanic's fluidity on the primary K–2 device is unknown. A laggy drag on a 2GB iPad is indistinguishable from a working one until a child plays it.
  * **Reset-device gesture** wraps a raw `db.delete()` with no audit log; a 2-step confirm is the only guard. Heavy and silent destruction one tap into a screen the persona shouldn't be on.
  * **Two duplicate scene implementations cause developer cognitive load** that surfaces as user-facing inconsistency: hint-copy lookups, feedback animations, and session-complete behaviour all risk diverging between L1 and L2+. Players will feel this as the app being uneven.
  * **No discoverable path to TTS for a non-reader.** A K–2 player looking for "the voice that reads to me" has no obvious affordance — the option lives behind a small gear icon. A first-tap signal (D-5) was deprioritised; its absence is felt.
