# Consent & Assent Scripts

Read aloud; no signature required (informal validation, not research).

---

## Parent consent script (~1 minute)

> "Hi — I'm Ryan. I made a short educational game about fractions for K–2 kids, and I'm testing whether it actually helps children learn. I'd love for [child's name] to try it today.
>
> A few things to know:
> — The session is about 40 minutes: a short pencil-and-paper warm-up, 20 minutes of free play, and a quick wrap-up.
> — All data stays on this device. Nothing is uploaded anywhere, and I won't record video or audio.
> — I'll take handwritten notes about how [child] interacts with the game, but no names go in those notes — just a participant number.
> — You can stop the session at any time for any reason, and that's completely fine.
>
> Do you have any questions? Are you okay with [child] trying it?"

Wait for verbal "yes" before proceeding.

---

## Child assent script (~30 seconds, K–2 vocabulary)

> "Hey! I made a game about fractions — you know, like cutting pizza into equal pieces.
> I want to see if it's fun and if it helps you learn.
> You can stop any time — just say 'I want to stop' and we stop, no problem.
> Want to give it a try?"

Wait for enthusiastic agreement. If the child seems uncertain, do not proceed.

---

## Ending the session early

If a child wants to stop:

> "That's totally okay! You did great. Here's your [sticker / thank-you]."

Do not pressure or negotiate. Record the stop time and reason in the observation rubric.
