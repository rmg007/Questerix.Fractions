# Phase 1–3 Pre/Post Test Instrument

**QUESTERIX FRACTIONS — Learning Outcome Assessment**

---

## Administration Instructions

**Duration:** ~5 minutes per student  
**Format:** Paper, administered 1-on-1 with child (not whole-class)  
**Scoring:** Correct/incorrect (binary); no partial credit  
**When to administer:**
- **Pre-test:** Before the student plays any Questerix session (baseline)
- **Post-test:** After the student completes 3 sessions over 2 weeks (Phase 3 only)

---

## Item Specification

Each item uses **bar models** (not symbolic notation) to avoid notation-specific biases. Items target the core learning hypothesis: students can visually identify fraction magnitude without explicit instruction.

### **Item 1: Identify Half (Visual)**
[VISUAL: Two circles, one partitioned into 2 equal parts with 1 shaded; one partitioned into 4 equal parts with 2 shaded.]

**Question:** "Which circle shows 1 half shaded?"
- A) Left circle [Correct]
- B) Right circle
- C) Neither

**Rationale:** Tests whether student can identify 1/2 visually (prerequisite for Phase 1).

---

### **Item 2: Identify Quarter (Visual)**
[VISUAL: Square partitioned into 4 equal parts; one part shaded.]

**Question:** "How many equal parts is this square cut into?"
- A) 2 parts
- B) 3 parts
- C) 4 parts [Correct]

**Rationale:** Tests visual partitioning count (denominator concept without symbols).

---

### **Item 3: Compare Halves (Same Denominator)**
[VISUAL: Two bar models side-by-side, both halves, but different wholes. Left: 1/2 of small rectangle. Right: 1/2 of large rectangle. Same size when drawn.]

**Question:** "Which shaded part is bigger?"
- A) Left [Correct — same visual size, same denominator]
- B) Right
- C) They are the same size

**Rationale:** Tests whether student compares numerator when denominators are equal (L6 concept).

---

### **Item 4: Compare Different Denominators (Whole-Number Bias Trap)**
[VISUAL: Two bar models. Left: 3 equal parts, 1 shaded (3/4). Right: 2 equal parts, 1 shaded (1/2). Both bars same total length.]

**Question:** "Which shaded part is bigger?"
- A) Left (3 parts, 1 shaded)
- B) Right (2 parts, 1 shaded) [Correct]
- C) They are the same size

**Rationale:** Tests MC-WHB-01 (whole-number bias). Student must resist picking larger numerator and instead compare area visually.

---

### **Item 5: Benchmark to Half**
[VISUAL: Number line 0–1 with midpoint marked as "half". Four fractions shown as points: 1/4, 1/2, 3/4, 1/8.]

**Question:** "Which fraction is closest to half?"
- A) 1/4
- B) 1/2 [Correct]
- C) 3/4
- D) 1/8

**Rationale:** Tests benchmark (L8 concept). Nominal presence on pre-test (student likely guesses); mastery expected on post-test if L8 is effective.

---

### **Item 6: Order Two Fractions (Same Numerator)**
[VISUAL: Two bar models. Left: 1/4 (1 part of 4). Right: 1/2 (1 part of 2). Both bars same scale.]

**Question:** "Which is smaller?"
- A) 1/4 [Correct]
- B) 1/2
- C) They are the same size

**Rationale:** Tests L7 (same-numerator comparison). Pre-test baseline; post-test should show mastery.

---

### **Item 7: Partition & Identify (Kinesthetic Analog)**
[VISUAL: Rectangle outline on paper with no lines. Instructions: "Draw lines to cut this rectangle into 3 equal parts. Shade 1 part."]

**Scoring:**
- Correct: 3 equal parts, 1 shaded, lines clearly separate parts
- Partially correct: 3 parts shaded correctly but lines are wobbly or unclear
- Incorrect: Wrong number of parts, parts not equal, or no partition

**Rationale:** Tests whether student can physically produce equal partitions (inverse of identification). Bridges symbolic and visual reasoning.

---

### **Item 8: Open-Ended Reasoning**
[NO VISUAL. Verbal question.]

**Question:** "If you have a pizza cut into 4 equal slices and you eat 1 slice, what fraction of the pizza did you eat?"
- A) 1/4 [Correct]
- B) 1/2
- C) 4
- D) "Don't know" [Acceptable if student hasn't been taught yet]

**Rationale:** Open-ended; tests whether student can translate a real-world scenario to fraction language (post-test: expect majority to choose A after Phase 3; pre-test: baseline is likely "don't know").

---

## Scoring Rubric

**Item scoring (1–8):**
- 1 point = correct answer selected or correct partition drawn
- 0 points = incorrect or no attempt

**Total possible:** 8 points

**Interpretation:**
- **0–2 points:** No fraction concept (baseline pre-test)
- **3–4 points:** Emerging (visual identification works, comparison inconsistent)
- **5–6 points:** Developing (identification and benchmark concepts present, ordering mixed)
- **7–8 points:** Proficient (all items correct; magnitude reasoning solid)

**Pre-test expectation:** Average ~2–3 points (kindergarten students with minimal prior instruction)  
**Post-test target (Phase 3):** Average ~6–7 points (growth of ~4 points over 3 sessions indicates learning)

## Notes for Administrators

- **Pacing:** Do not rush. Pause after each item to let student think. If a student seems confused, re-read the question once, then move on.
- **No hints:** Do not explain what fractions are or scaffold answers. This is an assessment, not a lesson.
- **Encourage:** Say "Good thinking" or "That's your best answer, thank you" regardless of correctness.
- **Visual aids:** Have bar models printed on cards or pre-drawn on the test paper itself. Large, clear images (at least 2 inches wide).
- **Item 7 preparation:** Provide blank rectangle on the test paper and a pencil. Let student draw freely; accept wobbly lines if intent is clear.

## Validation Evidence

**Alignment to CCSS:**
- Items 1–2: CCSS 1.G.A.3 (halves and fourths)
- Items 3–4: CCSS 2.NF.A.1 (comparing fractions)
- Item 5: CCSS 2.NF.A.3 (benchmarking)
- Items 6–7: CCSS 2.G.A.3 + 2.NF.A.1 (equal shares, comparison)
- Item 8: CCSS 2.NF.A.1 (fraction language)

**Misconception coverage:**
- Item 4 explicitly tests MC-WHB-01 (whole-number bias)
- Item 6 explicitly tests MC-WHB-02 (denominator bias)

## Printing Instructions

- **Format:** 8.5" × 11" paper, portrait orientation
- **Images:** Full-size bar models (no scaling down; large enough for a 5-year-old to see clearly)
- **Font:** 14pt sans-serif for questions, 18pt for correct/incorrect labels in answers
- **Copies:** Print one per student + 3 extras for practice/pilot
- **Lamination (optional):** Laminate if reusing for multiple cohorts; use dry-erase markers for Item 7
