# Pilot Protocol 1: Participant Consent & Parent Acknowledgment

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-05-05  
**Plan:** Plan 15 (pilot-protocol), Phase 1  
**Scope:** K–2 learner validation study, single-session interaction testing  

---

## 1. Study Overview

**Title:** Questerix Fractions — K–2 Interaction & Usability Pilot

**Objective:** Validate that K–2 learners can engage with fraction-learning interactions (partition, identify, compare) via magnetic-drag mechanics on tablets and desktops, and identify friction points in UX, accessibility, and pedagogical clarity.

**No IRB required:** This is a **formative validation study**, not a formal clinical trial or randomized experiment. Data collection is:
- Observational (does the child succeed?)
- Qualitative (where do they hesitate?)
- Not comparative (no control arm; no randomization)
- Non-invasive (standard tablet/browser gameplay)
- Data retention: **15 days post-session** (no ongoing data storage)

---

## 2. Participant Eligibility & Recruitment

### Who We Are Looking For
- **Age:** 5–8 years old (Kindergarten, Grade 1, Grade 2)
- **Math background:** Any level (no prior fraction exposure required)
- **Device familiarity:** Has used a tablet or touchscreen before
- **Language:** English-fluent (can follow on-screen prompts and verbal instructions)

### Who Is Excluded
- Students with diagnosed visual, hearing, or motor impairments (defer pilot until A11y layer fully validated; refer to educator support team if needed)
- Non-English speakers (no translator on-site; future localization phase)

### Recruitment Path
- **In-classroom:** With educator permission, recruit 1–2 students per session during math block
- **After-school:** Partner with school or library offering after-school STEM time
- **Home:** Family referral (parent brings child to testing location)

## 3. Parent/Guardian Acknowledgment Form

### Text

---

**PARENT/GUARDIAN ACKNOWLEDGMENT**

**Study:** Questerix Fractions — K–2 Interaction & Usability Pilot  
**Research Team:** Questerix Learning Team (questerix.learning@example.com)  
**Study Date:** [DATE]

---

**What is this study?**

We are testing a new educational game called Questerix Fractions. Your child will play one or more short game levels on a tablet or computer. Each level takes 5–10 minutes. We want to understand:

- Can your child understand the fraction concepts?
- Does the game work well on different devices?
- Where do kids get stuck or confused?
- Is the game fun?

**What will your child do?**

1. **Session intro (2 min):** I'll explain the game and what fraction is.
2. **Gameplay (up to 15 min):** Your child plays 2–3 game levels while I watch and take notes.
3. **Exit feedback (3 min):** I'll ask simple questions: "What was hard? What was fun?"

**Will my child be recorded?**

- **Video:** No.
- **Audio:** No.
- **Screen recording:** Yes — only the game screen (no camera, no audio). This helps us understand where kids click and how they solve problems. We erase it after 15 days.

**Will my child's name appear anywhere?**

- **No.** We assign each child a random ID (e.g., "Participant-042") on the day of the session.
- **Names, addresses, and ID numbers are never recorded** in the game or notes.

**What if my child is frustrated or wants to stop?**

- Your child can stop **anytime**. There is no penalty.
- If I see frustration, I pause and ask: "Would you like to take a break?" or "Should we try a different level?"
- Your child will get a sticker or small reward for trying (even if they finish or don't finish).

**Will the game change my child's homework or grades?**

- **No.** This game is separate from school grades. It is for us to learn, not to test your child.

**Who can see the notes and video?**

- Only the Questerix Learning Team and school educators involved in the pilot.
- We will **not** share notes or data with parents, other families, or the internet.
- Teachers may see a summary: "10 kids played Fractions today; most struggled with the fourths partition task."

**How is my child's privacy protected?**

- No real names, addresses, or birthdates in any notes or recordings.
- Screen recordings and notes are stored on a secure, encrypted device.
- After 15 days, all recordings and notes are deleted.
- We comply with COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act): no email signup, no accounts, no tracking.

**Can I contact the team with questions?**

Yes! Email questerix.learning@example.com or call [PHONE]. We respond within 24 hours.

## 4. Child Assent Script (Read Aloud by Facilitator)

*Use simple, friendly language; pause for questions.*

---

**"Hi [Child's First Name]! I'm so glad you're here today. We're testing a new game about fractions. Fractions are parts of shapes, like cutting a pizza into slices.**

**Here's what we're going to do:**

1. **I'll explain the game (2 minutes):** You'll see shapes on the screen. Your job is to cut them into equal parts or find which picture shows a certain number of parts.
2. **You'll play (10–15 minutes):** Try the game. If it's confusing, that's OK! I'm here to help. If you don't like a level, we can try a different one.
3. **I'll ask you some questions (3 minutes):** After, I'll ask you: 'What was fun? What was hard?'

**Important:**
- **You can stop anytime.** If you want to take a break or leave, just tell me. I won't be mad.
- **There are no wrong answers.** This is not a test. We're just learning how to make the game better.
- **You'll get a sticker** when we're done, whether you finish all the levels or not.

**Do you have any questions before we start?**"

## 7. End-of-Session Cleanup

**Immediately after the session:**

1. **Confirm notes:** Check that no child name, address, or ID appears in your notes (only "Participant-[#]").
2. **Stop recording:** End screen recording, mark timestamp.
3. **Deidentify notes:** Replace any accidental names with Participant ID.
4. **Reward:** Give sticker or small reward.
5. **Thank you:** Say "Thank you so much for helping us test the game!"
6. **Parent handoff:** If parent is present, say: "All done! We'll erase the video in 2 weeks. Any questions, email us."

**Data retention timeline:**
- Day 0: Session conducted, notes & video recorded
- Day 15: Automated deletion of all video files and identifiable notes
- Aggregate summary maintained (e.g., "L1 partition: 8/10 children succeeded")
